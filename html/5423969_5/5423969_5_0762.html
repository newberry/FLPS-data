Sixty men from Chicago were promoted to officers in the army by President Wilson, on the recommendation of the army department. Seven of the new officers are Scandinavians, and three of these are Norwegians.

The Norwegians appointed are Eivar B. Bergendahl, Captain; Trygue Siqueland, Captain; and Jens Doe, Lieutenant.

The new officers expect orders soon to leave for France and service at the front.
