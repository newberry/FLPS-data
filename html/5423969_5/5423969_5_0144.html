On the 17th day of May of which year the largest audience of Norwegians ever assembled in the United States gathered in the auditorium or some other large assembly hall in Chicago to do homage to the vital day of Norway's independence, and to keep alive the national spirit which was the distinctive work of the old Norse Vikings.

The old men who were born on the sacred soil of the midnight sun joined the younger generation of their American descendants in cheering as they listened to the familiar stirring national airs whose origin is now lost in the legends of the nation.
