Ludvig H. Lund was born in Kristiania April 8, 1870.

Lund started his career as journalist and writer at an early age. He traveled in continental Europe for several years, and came to Chicago at the age of twenty-one. He worked in various Scandinavian bookstores, such as Skandinaven's, and at John Relling's book store, Milwaukee and Chicago Avenues.

He was for many years editor of Scandia. Lund was, politically, a Socialist. He belonged to all the major Norwegian societies in Chicago.
