The Chicago Tuberculosis Institute will exhibit objects of instruction in the Pilsen Sokol park on March 26, 27, and 28. Preventive measures will be discussed by physicians and the evils of the "white plague" in Chicago will be demonstrated. Right habits of living will be an essential item of the lectures, and for this purpose Sokols will perform gymnastic drills and the physiological effect of such upon the body and mind, will be explained.

A musical program of violin music and solos will serve as an entertainment. The lectures will be varied for each evening, and be kept on the popular level. No admission will be asked of children during afternoon hours.
