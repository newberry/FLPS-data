In the preface of the Czech-English dictionary, the second edition of which appeared in 1896, Karel Jonas, the author, then living in Racine, Wis., expressed his hope that his work would establish a better understanding between his country-men and the people of their adopted country, the land of liberty.

The dictionary, now in its ninth edition, contains over 38,000 words with explanations, and 624 pages. Though the price of paper has almost doubled, the dictionary is still sold for $1.75, and may be obtained at Slavia, 2544 So. Millard Avenue, Chicago, Ill.
