The first signs of the dawning of a new day have appeared on our political horizon. For ages, this horizon has been clouded in heavy darkness. Through the breach which the election campaign has opened in the bastions of reaction, the first cabinet of the Left is about to enter to take charge of that reform work which so long has been neglected, owing to the egoism of the small group which has held power in Sweden. As with the Russian cossack, so with the Swedish reaction: where his horse's hoof has trod, grass cannot grow. And this reaction has trampled practically all over the political and social life in Sweden; everywhere it has left traces of destruction. As if a tornado had passed over the land transforming it into a desert 2from which the best forces have fled through mass emigration, and those remaining have become dwarfed. The old time Swedish freedom had been quelled so long that many had begun to despair of its regeneration.

Now, as we stated, the first signs of a new day are appearing, and new hope is surging through the people who have not yet lost their vitality. And indeed, it was time that something was done, it might appear that Sweden was to be out-distanced even by Russia. The dissolution of the union with Norway probably helped ot impress upon the Swedish people the fact that the ruling reactionary group had been permitted to go on too long with its destructive activity.

What is needed now is that the new liberal government should put its hands vigorously to the plow, not looking backward. The Fields which under the old regime were 3laid waste are of such enormous extent that it will require a long time and much labor to regain what is lost. And first of all, the new movement requires new tools, first of these, of course, is the universal vote, which the Swedish people, weighed down by taxes and military burdens, have waited for so long. In the field of legislation and administration, the old heaps of anachronisms are piled up. Here the task of renovating and of new construction will be enormous.....

In a word, the new government must become a government of the Left, not merely in name but in fact....It will not do any longer to waiver in the course. The results of the election clearly indicate the presence of a determined people with definite aims....The fight against the reactionary forces of an oligarchic government must be carried on vigorously; new ways 4of development must be designed for the building up of a new Sweden where every free citizen can feel at home.
