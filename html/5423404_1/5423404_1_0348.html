The Norwegians in Chicago are preparing to send a petition to President Roosevelt. The petition will be signed by about twenty-thousand, it is said. At the head of the undertaking are. Banker H. A. Haugan; Editor-in-Chief N. Grevstad of Skandinaven; Mayor F. H. Gude, Lake Forest; Banker Paul O. Stensland. A special delegation will go to Washington today, Tuesday, to place the petition in the hands of the President.

The contents of the petition briefly are: that in view of the latest differences between Norway and Sweden, the Norwegians of Chicago hope and wish that President Roosevelt will, as soon as possible, recognize the new constituted government in Norway, as accepted by the Norwegian parliament.
