Attorney Carl R. Chindblom, who was elected County Commissioner at last Tuesday's election, was born in Chicago in 1870, and received his early education in the public schools of this city. Later he attended Augustana College, Rock Island, from which he graduated with honors in 1890.

In 1894 he appeared as a political speaker in Michigan, and during the National campaigns in 1896 and 1900 he was engaged as a speaker by the Republican National Committee. He took up the study of law at Kent College of Law, Chicago, in 1897, passed the bar examinations in the spring of 1900, and began to practice immediately thereafter.

In 1903 he became a partner in the law firm of Deneen and Hamill, and has since last January acted as attorney for the State's Health Commission.
