In 1838 there were two Norwegians in Chicago. In 1905 Christiania was the only Norwegian city larger than Chicago.

Johan Larsen (sailor) and Holstein Thorsteinson, settled where Northwestern Depot now is in 1836.

David Johnson, a Norwegian sailor was first pressman in Chicago, 1834.

In 1848 a Norwegian Evangelical church was started.

By 1905 there were some 65,000 Norwegians but they did not exert themselves for political preferment.
