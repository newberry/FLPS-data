At a meeting held in Chicago, a few days ago, a committee of six men was formed for the purpose of organizing "The Society for the Advancement of Scandinavian Study". The purpose of the Society is explained by its name. Forty delegates, representing colleges from Harvard to Kansas, including several Scandinavian colleges, were present.

The following officers were elected: Professor Julius C. Olsen, president; Professor Jules Mauritzen, vice-president; Professor George T. Flom, secretary-treasurer; Dr. A. Louis Elmquist, Mr. Ernst Olsen, executive committee.
