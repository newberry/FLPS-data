On September 9, at the conclusion of the exercises held in Humboldt Park, where the Franco-Polish delegation addressed a tremendous crowd of Poles at the Kosciusko monument, a reception for the delegation was held at the Polish Press Club, at which the delegates, representatives of Polish organizations, and prominent citizens of American Polonia were present. The following attended:

J. F. Smulski was toastmaster. The first address was made by Stefan Rejer, representative of the coal mines in Poland, who spent more than twenty-five years among the coal miners of Westphalia and who has recently worked with the miners in Northern France. He has already lost two sons in the present war and was himself wounded.

[Address by Rejer omitted by translator].

Following Rejer's address, Mrs. M. Milewski, member of the board of directors of the Polish National Alliance, assured him in behalf of the Women's Division 3that clothing for the Polish coal miners would continue to be sent in quantities proportionate to the extent of our means.

Other speeches were made by the Reverend Duzynski, who spoke in French, to which the delegate from the French government replied, speaking of the mutual aims of the Allied powers and of Poland's future; John Wedda, who spoke as representative of the Poles born on this soil, and I. Osostowicz, editor of Dziennik Zwiazkowy, who also spoke in French in behalf of the Polish newspapermen.

During the course of the banquet, telegrams were received from Bishop Paul Rhode, Ignace Paderewski, and from Circuit I of the Polish Falcons' Alliance, New York.

The banquet lasted until ten o'clock in the evening, and after it was concluded the company spent several hours more in pleasant conversation.

This evening, the Franco-Polish delegation will leave for Cambridge Springs, Pennsylvania, where it will attend the exercises opening the school term at the Polish National Alliance Academy.
