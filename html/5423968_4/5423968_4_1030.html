In the Chicago City Council, the Republican friends of monopolies and other wise speculators are not asleep to their opportunities.

Here is a sample of their cleverness.

According to Mr. Peter Kiolbassa, an ordinance has been introduced in the Chicago City Council which, if passed, would cause a lot of uneasiness to our Polish coal dealers. This ordinance, which provides for the payment of fifty dollars annually as a license fee, has been referred to the Law Committee of the City Council.

It is evidently a threat directed against the owners of small coal yards, who find it difficult to earn a more subsistence. It wants to take away this small profit and give it to the large companies and monopolies.

2

For that reason this ordinance should be fought by all honest citizens.

Mr. Peter Kiolbassa informed us that he is opposed to this ordinance and will fight it energetically.

Perhaps our Polish coal dealers will want to help him in this fight. A petition should be prepared and submitted to the City Council, etc. Those interested in this matter would do well to get in touch with Mr. Kiolbassa.

At any rate, we are bringing this to the attention of our Polish coal dealers.
