One of the leading Polish enterprises in the United States is the White Eagle Brewing Company, organized on the 21st of November, 1899, by the late John F. and Francis Czaja, Adam Czaja, son of John F., and Bernard and Anna Maciejewski. Originally, the brewery was located near St. Adalbert's church at 18th street and Ashland avenue, the present location of Pulaski Hall, which is still owned by the brewery.

EXPANSION OF THE BREWERY

In 1907 the White Eagle Brewing Company bought out the Illinois Brewing and Malting Company, located at 3735-35 S. Racine avenue. As soon as the transaction was completed, the White Eagle Brewing Company moved in. At the same time the company reorganized an incorporated, accepting only Poles as its shareholders.
