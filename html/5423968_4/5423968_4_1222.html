Sister Poles!

Loyal to and imbued with an ardent love for our unhappy country, Poland, we have at the same time obligations of gratitude toward the magnificent, free, and independent land of America. Some of us have come here exiled from our native land by the might of our enemies; others of us were born in America, but all of us have found liberty, protection, and bread here. The powerful United States is, therefore, as another homeland to us.

The time is coming when America will demand of its citizens proof of their love and affection. In order that this moment may not find these citizens unprepared, American women are organizing committees in order to help the country.

We Polish women who owe America a debt of gratitude must not withdraw from 2serving the nation, but should stand ready to work together with the daughters of this country.

Let us follow the shining examples of our Polish heroes, who generously shed their blood in defense of America's right to freedom.

For this purpose, the Women's Auxiliary of the Polish National Alliance is hereby convoking a general mass meeting for Sunday, February 11, at 2 P. M., at the Polish National Alliance Building, 1406-08 West Division Street, to which it cordially invites all Polish women, regardless of what organizations they belong to, in order to discuss the problem together and to organize a service committee. In this way we will express honorably and fittingly the feelings we have for the people and the country, whose representative President Wilson announced before the whole world that Poland should be united, free, and autonomous.

Every Polish woman who is a loyal American citizen is obliged to attend this 3mass meeting.
