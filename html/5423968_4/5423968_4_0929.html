Representative Barbour has introduced in the State legislature of Illinois a bill prohibiting the sale of liquor at social functions and excursions, regardless of whether they take place on Sundays, weekdays, or holidays.

This is not only a plot against the personal liberty of the people of this country, but also a threat against the existence of fraternal aid societies, which strengthen their treasuries for aid to the sick by arranging social functions. This is the only source of income our societies have, an income without which they simply cannot exist.

Protests should be made at once against this plot, which is against personal liberty and threatens the existence of our societies. The officers of Polish societies in Illinois should get together immediately and send protests to their district senators and representatives.
