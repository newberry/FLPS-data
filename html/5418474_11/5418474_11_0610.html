Immigration from Europe increases steadily. In May, this year, 18,328 immigrants arrived in New York, an approximate increase of 7,000 over the same month last year. Total immigration since January 1, 1879, for New York reached 40,592--an increase of 12,447 over the same period last year.

The Immigration Commissioners of New York declare that the arrivals of this year are highly desirable; most of them are farmers or skilled workers.

Communistic rowdies seem to be almost wanting.
