Mr. Wilhelm Buschmann, one of the oldest German settlers on the Southside, died last night at his home 3945 State Street. Mr. Buschmann, who was born in Germany, came as a young man to America and lived for some years as a prospector in the West. He finally struck gold and came back to Chicago, a rich man. He invested his wealth in real estate and leaves a fortune of at least $700,000, which are going to his relatives. Buschmann never was married and died a bachelor.
