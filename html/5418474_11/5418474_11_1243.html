Prominent and well known among German-Americans, Walter W. L. Meyer, was born June 23, 1892, in Chicago. Meyer comes from a family which preserved its German sentiment for generations. The parents of Mr. Meyer were also born here, in Chicago. The family originally immigrated from Mecklenburg, Germany.

Walter Meyer attended public schools here, later studied law at Northwestern University and the University of Illinois. As a young man he was elected assistant State's Attorney under Maclay Hoyne, and finally in 1922 became a reviewer in the circuit court. Furthermore, he occupies a professional chair of jurisprudence, at Loyola University. He is a member of the Germania Club, vice-president of the Steuben Club, and a member of numerous other German-American societies and clubs.
