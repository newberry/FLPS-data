Julius Goldzier, the chairman of the Chicago Turngemeinde Committee that leads the movement for introduction of the Postal Savings System has received a letter from Samuel Gompers, the President of the American Federation of Labor, in which the support of this organization is promised. The National Representative Foss of this City wrote to Mr. Goldzier, that in a Labor Day speech in 1897 or 1898 he declared himself in favor of introduction of Postal Savings Banks and that he still maintains his view point.

Also from many other sides the Committee has received satisfying letters.
