The shrinking row of old settlers has lost another member, Mrs. Anna Martha Best who died yesterday.

She was born in 1818 in Hesson Darmstadt, (Germany) and came to New York in 1835, where she married. In 1839 the couple came to Chicago and started, a vinegar factory, at 14th Street and Indiana Ave., which was a success and later became a brewery. The business prospered well and later on, in 1850, was bought up by Conrad Seipp.

Mrs. Anna Martha Best was known for her kindness and humanitarian activity. She is leaving behind 5 sons, Henri, Martin, Charles, John, Frederick and 2 married daughters. Mrs. Niggermeyer and Mrs. F.H. Fregel.

The funeral will be held Sunday at 10 A. M.
