A mass meeting of unemployed workers was held Thursday evening, January 27, to protest against the Chicago police, who split the heads of many unemployed workers when they attempted to start a parade at Hull House on January 17.

Among the speakers was Lucy Parsons, who was one of those arrested as a result of the riot on January 17. Wm. Thurston Brown, an ex-priest and secretary of the Colorado State Socialist Party, also spoke.

All those who had been arrested denounced the Chicago police as the lowest kind of human beings.

Brown said: "I would rather see my son die than see him wear the uniform of a policeman." He also placed priests in the same category with the police.

Rev. Irwin Tucker and Vincent St. John were also scheduled to speak, but they2 failed to appear at the meeting.

A resolution was adopted, denouncing the nefarious deeds of the police.

A large crowd attended the protest meeting in spite of the severe cold weather.
