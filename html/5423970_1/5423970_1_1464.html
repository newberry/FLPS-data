A group of Chicago Lithuanians, led by Mr. P. Mulevicius and Mr. S. Drangelis, have completed arrangements to leave Chicago on Tuesday, January 19, for the Southern States. Members of the party hope to find a suitable location in the South for the establishment of a Lithuanian farm colony. They plan to stop at Dodson, Louisiana. From there they will visit and survey other nearby points.

After the party succeeds in finding a suitable location and settles down, they plan to invite other Lithuanians to join them.
