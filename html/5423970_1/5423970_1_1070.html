A gigantic Fourth Liberty Loan parade, in which Lithuanians participated, was held in the downtown district last Saturday.

The Lithuanians had their own float, which had been beautifully decorated under the supervision of the artist, J. Sileika.

The float pictured a war-torn home, with a mother and her little children calling for help to withhold the threatened stroke of the Kaiser's sword. Uncle Sam, as the protector of the oppressed, restrains the Kaiser.

The float made a great impression on the public.

The Lithuanians had two good bands in the parade.
