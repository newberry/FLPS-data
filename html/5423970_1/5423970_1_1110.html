After the completion of the Third Liberty Loan campaign, the foreign nationality divisions which were active in selling Liberty bonds in Chicago held a meeting.

About two hundred people, representing thirty nationalities, attended the meeting, which was held at the Morrison Hotel. The representatives and managers of the Lithuanian division, Mr. J. Elias and Mr. John I. Bagdziunas, and several other Lithuanians also attended.

All of the national groups together sold three hundred and forty million dollars' worth of Liberty bonds. In the Seventh Federal Reserve District, Mr. Elias reported, the Lithuanians collected more than one million four hundred thousand dollars.
