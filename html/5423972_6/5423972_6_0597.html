At a banquet last night at the Hotel LaSalle, the United Charities celebrated its first year of existence.

Julius Rosenwald, the president of the Jewish Charities, and chairman of the meeting, declared that one of the biggest events of the year was the $2,500,000 drive. Dr. Louis L. Mann, Rabbi of Sinai Temple, was the main speaker of the evening.

The following officers were installed for the year of 1924:- Honorary President, Julius Rosenwald; President, Charles Rubin; Vice-President, Sol Klein; Treasurer, A. Foreman; Secretary, Marcy Berger.

The charities distributed more than $1,500,000 to its affiliated institutions.
