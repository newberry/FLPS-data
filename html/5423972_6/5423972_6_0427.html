"King" Levinsky, the famous Jewish fighter of Maxwell Street, won a decision yesterday evening from former champion Jack Dempsey, with whom he fought four rounds.

Levinsky was very impressive, having practically the upper hand over Dempsey throughout the skirmish.

Billie Burke, in charge of the box office, declared, that twenty-three thousand enthusiasts packed the Stadium. Gross receipts amounted to $75,000.

Both fighters, Levinsky and Dempsey, weighed in yesterday afternoon on the scales at the offices of the Illinois Athletic Commission.

Levinsky's weight of 196 1/2 lbs. was 6 1/2 lbs. more than Dempsey's.

Dempsey's weight was 190 lbs. This is 2 1/2 lbs, less than when the former champion weighed four and one half years ago in his championship fight with Gene Tunney of Sept. 22, 1927.
