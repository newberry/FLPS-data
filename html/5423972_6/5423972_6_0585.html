A special meeting of the Board was called in tribute to A. G. Becker, whose death occurred on May 14, James Davis delivered a eulogy on Mr. Becker. "For a quarter of a century he was the most outstanding figure in the Jewish Community of Chicago. He was one of the organizers and one of the directors and trustees at the inception of the organization in 1900. In 1902 and 1902 he was selected vice-president of the Board. In the years 1903-4-5 and again in 1913 he was chairman of the Board and it is due to his sagacity and untiring efforts that the J. C. has become a model of efficiency in it's field of endeavor".
