A number of educational lectures on American habits, conditions, general life, and American acquisitions are to be held in the Czechoslovak Republic. In order that these educational lectures may be more valuable, slides will be used. Mr. Emil Tuma, editor of the Americka Korespondence (American Correspondence), 606 Staromestske Namesti, Prague, Czechoslovakia, was authorized to collect the necessary material, such as pictures from various periodicals, daily newspapers, etc. All such material should be sent to him. A good deed will be accomplished by anyone who sends such pictures and illustrations to Mr. Tuma, because by it he will help to inform our people about American ways of life.

The following pictures and illustrations are needed:

Streets: Street sweeper, sprinkling of streets, machine and water sweeping, sewer cleaning, sanitary inspection, street paving and repair.

Buildings: Safety devices, erection of skyscrapers, house moving.

Street traffic: Parades, funerals, circus parade, playgrounds, and games.

Parks: Park conveniences, lawns, drinking fountains, refuse boxes playgrounds, zoological gardens.

Railroads: Freight locomotive, passenger locomotive, railroad depot entrance, depot interior, information desk, ticket office, safety devices, trainmaster, porter, down town ticket office, country railroad crossing, passenger coach, sleeping coach, dining coach, service.
