Every Czech and Slovak rejoiced over the decision of President Wilson, by which the Czechoslovak nation is declared free, and on a par with the other Allied nations. Each one, regardless of how much he helped the cause, is overjoyed, and considers every act of our President a wise measure designed to bring about a victorious ending of the war.

We should not find fault with his executive orders--not even with his latest, although it sounds the knell for our beloved beverage, beer.

Considering the quality, however, in which beer is dispensed at present, it will not be missed very much.
