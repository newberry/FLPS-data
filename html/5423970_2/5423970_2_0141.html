A concert was given by the teachers of the Beethoven Conservatory of Music last Sunday, March 7, at the St. George's [Lithuanian] Parish Hall, 32nd Place and Auburn [now Lituanica] Avenue. Those who participated in the program were: Mr. A. A. Aleksandravicius, Mr. P. Bartkevicius, Mrs. Ona Pocius, Mr. J. Jakaitis, and Mr. Anthony Pocius, who is the director of the Conservatory.

Mr. A. Aleksandravicius played the following numbers on the piano: "Nocturne," by Liszt; "Oi Eisim, Mes Cia Nebusim" (We Will Go, Go, And Not Stay Here); and "Concert Waltz in E Flat," by Fieldhouse. He displayed a lack of smoothness in some parts, but on the whole was very good.

Mr. Bartkevicius, who played a few numbers on the clarinet, displayed good technique and tone. Mrs. Pocius demonstrated her usual talent as a singer. Mr. Jakaitis sang "Bezode Daina" (Song Without Words), and was called back by the audience for an encore. The technique of Mr. Pocius, who is an accomplished 2pianist, was as usual very good. The concert ended with the singing of "Ave Maria" by the Bach-Gounod Trio.

Besides the musical program, a lecture on the subject "The Cultural Aspects of Music" was delivered by Mr. J. A. Chmieliauskas, who is a member of the editorial staff of Lietuva. Generally speaking, the concert was a success, and in quality stands out above all our so-called "concerts". The attendance was not large.
