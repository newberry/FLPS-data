A Lithuanian vaudeville troupe, consisting of three men and two women, was formed last Friday at a meeting of Chicago Lithuanian theatrical artists in the Milda Theater hall, 3140 South Halsted Street. This troupe is the first of a series that is being planned for the purpose of creating a continuous and permanent Lithuanian vaudeville theater in Chicago.

Ten of the most popular Chicago Lithuanian dramatic artists attended the meeting. They all enthusiastically endorsed the idea of establishing a permanent Lithuanian vaudeville theater, and promised to help in every possible way.

The next meeting of the artists will take place on Friday, May 29, at the Lithuanian Theater, 3214 South Halsted Street.
