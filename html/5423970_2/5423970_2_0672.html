The Chicago district of the Lithuanian-American Workers Literary Society gave a very interesting musical entertainment, Feb. 21, at the Chicago Lithuanian Auditorium. Besides Lithuanians, artists of other nationalities paricipated in the program. A capacity crowd attended.

A number of solos and duets were sung by the following Lithuanian artists: Miss Meldazis, Miss Elena Peciukas, Paul Dauderis, and Ona Pocius. Many were asked to repeat their numbers, especially Ona Pocius, who pleased the audience immensely with her highly developed 2voice. She returned from Lithuania only two weeks ago. In Lithuania she was a popular member of the Lithuanian State Opera. She proved at the concert that her popularity is well deserved.

Mr. Rodella, famous Italian tenor, also made a fine impression.

The Jewish Freiheit Chorus, which is a real novelty in Lithuanian programs, electrified the audience with its fine performance. The chorus delivered many encores before the audience finally consented to allow it to leave the stage.

During an intermission period, Comrade J. Gasiunas delivered a short talk on the aims of the Lithuanian-American Workers Literary Society. He invited those present to join the society.
