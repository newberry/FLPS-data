The First Chapter (Chicago) of the Aurora Society is sponsoring a long list of lectures. The lectures will be read every Sunday, beginning at 3 P.M., at the Davis Square Hall, 45th Street and Marshfield Avenue.

October 9: K. Sliupas, "A Modern History of the French Revolution." (This lecture will be given at the J. Elias Hall, 4600 So. Wood Street.)

October 16: K. Sliupas, "A Modern History of Napoleonic Times."

October 23: Dr. A. L. Graiciunas, "Sickness and Health."

October 30: J. J. Hertmanavicius, "The Manufacture of Wealth."

November 6: K. Jurgelionis, "The Theatrical Arts: Declamation, Mimicry, and Acting."

November 13: J. P. Varkala, "How Much People Spend on Drink in the World, in America, and Especially in Town of Lake."

November 20 and 27: K. Sliupas, "The New History."

December 4: Dr. F. Matulaitis' lecture, "How to Keep One's Health," will be read by Dr. A. L. Graiciunas.

December 11: K. Jurgelionis, "The Language of the Apes and the Beginning of Languages."

December 18: Dr. A. L. Graiciunas, "The Causes of Contagious Diseases and the Fight Against Them."

Some of the lectures will be illustrated. Admission will be free.
