On June 28, Anthony Pocius and his wife, Ona, conducted a concert by the students of their school, the Beethoven Conservatory of Music. The concert was held at St. George's parish hall, 32nd Place and Auburn [now Lituanica] Avenue. Both children and grownups participated in the program; there were about eight children, mostly girls, and sixteen adults.

The program consisted of simple piano, violin, and vocal selections. All participants in the program gave excellent performances. The pianists and singers received bouquets.

Of the children, Vytautas Briedis, a ten-year-old Lithuanian boy, made the best impression; he distinguished himself not so much by good technical delivery as by his courage and self-reliance. He gave the impression that he felt entirely at home, like an old trouper, as he played a number on the piano...He did not receive any flowers, but the great applause of the audience brought him out on the stage a second time; this is really worth more to 2him than any bouquet of flowers.

The program was concluded with a number of songs by a mixed chorus of sixteen singers under the direction of Mrs. Ona Pocius. The chorus sang Mikas Petrauskas' composition "Dega Ugnis" (The Fire Is Burning) and Rossini's aria "Inflammatus"; Miss Ona Rudauskas, a soprano, sang the solo. The chorus made a fine impression on the audience.

After the program, Mr. Pocius, director of the Conservatory, distributed medals of distinction among the younger pupils of his school, which is located at 3259 South Halsted Street.
