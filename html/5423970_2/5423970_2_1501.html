On October 29, the Lovers of the Motherland Society, branch 127, sponsored its first lecture. Mr. J. Laukis from Chicago, read a lecture on "Prostitution at Present and in the Past." The speaker explained how this horrible business could be abolished.

After the lecture, Mr. Laukis gave a short talk on the subject of what benefit this literary society is bringing to the Lithuanian public. A nine-year old boy, Demskis, recited two poems: "The Bell," and "The Motherland."
