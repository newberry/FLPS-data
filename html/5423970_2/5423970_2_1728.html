Good catholics and loyal countrymen gathered to guard themselves from worry and misfortune, that during illness or disaster they could get benefit and brotherly aid, to protect themselves against godlessness and countrylessness. They decided to organize a Benefit Society, in which all good Lithuanians and catholics could unite and work as one. On Rev. 10, 1910 the first meeting was held in the St. Michael's Parish Hall. The nine people that were there talked calmly and unanimously worked to organize a society and call it St. Joseph Guardian Society. The first board was elected at that me thing. The president, A. Sapitavicius; secretary, J. Krutkevicius, and others.

At the second meeting the famous Rev. N. Lukosius joined the society along with 22 more members. The society grew with speed and many joined, but a charter had not seen secured from the government yet. Therefore, at the second meeting a committee was selected to get a charter. The committee consisted of J. Zivatkauskas and J. Krukevicius.

At the third meeting a most important committee was selected: one to make a constitution for the society. The constitution was made on a firm catholic 2basis, free thinkers were not to be admitted. Thus, organized the society grew and strengthened, signifying big step forward.

From the time of the first meeting the Society has given 19 picnics and outings,--about 26 concerts and dances, always enlarging their treasury, to prepare for future misfortunes of their members in time of illness and disaster.

All the profits have been used for the good of the society's members church, and native land.

The society pays for a holy mass in honor of her dead members every year.

The society, gives Honor Rings each year to the members who haven't been ill for ten years. Also, the society, contributes to the church. The St. Michael's Parish Church was given $375.00.

Lithuania, fighting for independence, sought aid from American Lithuanians. The society's members, loving their Nation and Fatherland didn't refuse to 3help her and donated $460.00. In order to take care of Lithuania's wounded soldiers, fighters for Lithuanian Freedom, the society gave $25.00 to the Lithuanian Red Cross.

For upholding and widening the catholic press, the society bought $50.00 worth of shares of the Daily News, "Friend." The society who contributed to the Lithuanian fund for issuance of natural currency.

A sick member of the society is paid $5.00 a week as benefit. When a member dies, $50.00 is paid from the treasury, and each member gives one dollar.

Now there are about 70 members.

V. Rekus, 1639 N. Wood St.
