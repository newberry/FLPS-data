A new women's group, the "Daughters of the Lithuania Benefit Society" was formed in Bridgeport last Sunday. Married and single women can belong to the Society. The fees: twenty-five cents a month; one dollar for initiation. About forty-five joined the Society at the beginning. The new Society immediately elected the following officers: K. Chmieliauskiene, president; S. Miniat, vice-president; E. Juzaitiene, recording secretary; M. Zuraitiene, treasurer. Financial matters will be supervised by two secretaries: H. Carney, elected as first secretary and W. Pajauckiene, as second secretary. The new officers took to their work very energetically and promise to make this Society the largest Lithuanian women's organization not only in Chicago, but in America.
