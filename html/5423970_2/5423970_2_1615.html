The Truth Loving National Society held its first meeting June 24, 1897 at 749 West 22nd Street. John Chmeliauskas explained the aim of the society at the meeting. After a brief discussion it was decided to organize a national independent society, named the Lithuanian National Society of Truth Lovers. The first president was Alexandra Byanskas, the secretary, John Chmeliauskas. Seventeen members joined the society at the first meeting. The income of the first meeting was $6.25.

The Society's Aim

1. The Truth Loving Society had for its aim--to help her members in time of sickness and in case of poverty and to have brotherly love toward each other.

2

2. The Society, to the best of its ability, will try to give national parades and celebrate the more important Lithuanian national holidays; give national shows, etc.

3. All the Society's doings, both oral and written, must be done in the Lithuanian language.

Dues

Each member of the Society pays 25c a month in the sick benefit fund, and at the beginning of a year each member pays $3.00 for the death fund. If it happens that there isn't enough money in the death fund, then each member is proportionally assessed to get the required amount for the death fund.

Benefit in Sickness and Death

Every member having been in the Society six months and having fully paid his dues, will in time of sickness receive $5.00 a week for twelve weeks, but not until fourteen days after his illness. Therefore, a member who has his sick 3benefit for twelve weeks, either at one time or at intervals, cannot get benefit in time of sickness for the next three years.

The Society pays $250.00 to the family of a dead member.

The Society's Activity

July 3, 1897. It was decided to protest against the newspaper, The Sun, because it had slandered the Society.

December 1897. The year's income in the treasury was $88.25. Output $31.30. Profit left $56.95.

November 13, 1898. It was decided to donate $5.00 from the treasury for Demskis' book. (Priest Demskis was Roman Catholic priest who later became very active against religion. He wrote several anti-religion books. A. Ambrose)

4

May 14, 1899. There was a protest against Rev. Kriauciunas for his vile conduct toward the Lithuanians.

June 11, 1899. $5.00 was donated for the parade in Paris in 1900 - the Lithuanian branch.

February 11, 1900. Lietuva (The Lithuania) was taken for an organ of the Society, and the members were to pay $1.00 a year, subscription.

December 14, 1902. The organ of the Society was changed to Katalinas (The Catholic), and the price was raised for Lietuva.

August 9, 1906. $50.00 was donated for the Lithuanian-Russian revolution.

September 14, 1906. It was decided to take part in the Lithuanian Historical Simano Dakota anniversary parade, October 28, 1906.

March 10, 1912. $25.00 was donated for the Lawrence weaver strikers.

5

March 11, 1913. It was decided to free Paul Zilvitis, a student, from all dues.

May 11, 1913. It was decided to participate in the Lithuanian National Cemetery consecration. $10.00 was donated.

December 13, 1914. $50.00 was donated from the treasury for the Lithuanian National Cemetery.

January 10, 1915. $25.00 was donated for the Lithuanian war veterans.

March 14, 1915. $50.00 was donated for the Lithuanian National Cemetery.

August 10, 1916. Two lots were bought in the Lithuanian National Cemetery.

August 10, 1916. It was decided to join in helping the Lithuanian war veterans and collect donations for the Special Day on November 1, 1916.

6

March 11, 1917. $25.00 was donated to the war veterans.

July 8, 1917. It was decided to have a parade in honor of the Society, which had been organized now for twenty years.

December 9, 1917. $25.00 was donated to the Children's Day Nursery Society.

February 10, 1918. $25.00 was donated to the Fatherland Lovers Society, so they would help publish L. Serna's writings.

February 10, 1918. It was decided to join in the New York Congress affair in the month of March and to send a delegate.

April 14, 1918. It was decided to participate in the Chicago Lithuanian parade on April 28.

April 14, 1918. It was decided to buy American Liberty Bonds for $1,000.00.

7

August 11, 1918. The Truth Loving National Society decided to join the Alliance Society of Bridgeport.

March 9, 1919. It was decided to free the society members who had returned from the war from dues for the time they had been to war.

April 13, 1919. It was decided to join in the building of a hall in Bridgeport by buying $1,000 worth of shares. The members promised to buy 82 shares -$820.00.

May 11, 1919. It was decided to donate $42.00 for the Lithuanian National Cemetery.

August 14, 1919. It was decided to join the Chicago Lithuanian Council.

October 10, 1920. It was decided to join in the parade on October 31, which protested against Poland's attempt to seize Lithuania.

8

May 12, 1920. $100.00 was donated to help Lithuania. Also the members donated $51.25.

December 11, 1921. $1,000.00 was loaned to the Lithuanian National Cemetery for the purpose of installing water.

October 8, 1922. It was decided to participate in the Vilno 600th anniversary Jubilee Parade.

November 12, 1922. Delegates from the Bridgeport Alliance Society reported that the following spring the Alliance Society would build a hall which would cost about $150,000.00.

December 10, 1922. $1,000.00 worth of shares were bought for the Chicago Lithuanian Auditorium.

December 9, 1923. $10.00 was donated from the treasury for the Mariampoles Reales High School. The members gave $20.00.

9

March 9, 1924. It was decided to protest against the limitation of immigration.

December 14, 1924. It was decided to buy Chicago Lithuanian Auditorium Gold Bonds for $1,000.00.

April 12, 1925. It was decided to buy more Chicago Lithuanian Auditorium shares for $230.00.

February 13, 1937. A letter was received from the St. Casimir's Convent sisters, asking for a donation. After a brief discussion it was decided not to donate.

August 9, 1928. A letter was received from the Holy Cross Hospital Building Fund, asking for a donation. After a brief discussion it was decided not to donate.

The following societies joined the Truth Loving National Society:

10

1. The Bishop Valancauskas Society joined in 1934 with about 120 members.

2. The Bridgeport Political Club joined in 1934 with about 130 members.

3. The Rosary Society joined in 1935 with about 40 members.

4. The Lithuanian Daughters Society joined in 1936 with about 60 members.

At the present time the society has 589 members. It has $21,695.00 worth of property.
