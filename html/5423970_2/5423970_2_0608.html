The first meeting of this organization was held on November 14, 1911, at the Stancik Hall, 205 E. 115th St.

Mr. Z. Jucaitis called the meeting to order, explained the purpose of this meeting and urged the people to spread the dramatic art among the Lithuanians of Roseland by organizing a Drama Circle. After discussing broadly such necessity, the Lithuanian Drama Lovers' Circle of Roseland was organized. It was decided that the Lithuanian Scenic Art Circle must be impartial; it must deal only with the propagation of the dramatic art among Lithuanians. Also, at this meeting it was decided that the initiation fee should be one dollar for men, while the women should be admitted free, and that they should pay no other dues.

\2

The first members to join this Drama Circle and pay their dues were Z. Jusaitis, B. Ludkevicia and F. Skrebutenas. The first administration was: President, L. Jusaitis; Secretary, B. Ludkevicia; Treasurer, Miss O. Klimaviciute.

At this meeting it was decided to present the first performance, "Mr. Felix's Visitation," on December 25, 1911, at the Stancik Hall. In presenting this play the following persons will take part: A. Narbutas, Miss J. Aleksandraviciute, B. Ludkevicius, Z. V-tis, and D-ne.

Short Sketch.

The Lithuanian Drama Lovers Circle was organized in the year of 1911. During the last 26 years it has been working in the theatrical field. For a long time the Circle was the greatest drama power in Roseland, and this cultural work was done with great devotion.

Only in recent years, when the depression came, has the Circle suffered and its activities stopped. The members of the Circle have worked in other societies, even though the circumstances were unfavorable.

During its existence, the Scene Circle (sic) performed sixty-nine plays. For various societies it performed thirty-seven plays, for which it received about $1,000. For the Circle itself there were performed twenty-eight plays; seventeen of the performances gave a profit of $800, while seven performances gave a deficit of $200. From all the Circle's performances the profit was $600. Several

\22

plays were performed free (of charge) for the benefit of the Aurora Library.

This year the idea was brought forth of liquidating the Drama Lovers Circle, but after discussion it was decided to leave it as it is and try to renew its activities.

Many of the organizers still are active in the Circle. If any of them quit the Scene Lovers Circle (sic), they were forced to do so by the living conditions. The old members that still belong to the Circle are B. Liudkevicius, A. Narbutas, A. Jocius, J. Grybas, Mr. and Mrs. Dambrauskas, the singer Mrs. Pazarskiene, Griskenas, K. Baronas, Vitkus, Andrijauskas, S. Diluis, and others.
