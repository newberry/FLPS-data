p.8..............The first district of the American Lithuanian Workers Society held a conference on January 3, at the Vilnis Hall, 3116 South Halsted Street. Seventy delegates, representing twenty one chapters, and about one-thousand members attended the conference.

Delegates from all chapters delivered reports before the conference, describing the various activities of each chapter. It was one of the most lively conferences ever held by the district.

According to a report by the district chairman, the district did not increase its membership during the past year. He said the recent new membership drive was devoted mainly to spreading working class literature, especially getting new subscribers to the Laisve (Liberty), Lithuanian Communist daily newspaper in New York.
