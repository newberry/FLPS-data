The White Rose Lithuanian Athletic Club, of the Town Of Lake district, is making plans to build a clubhouse.

The Club has purchased a lot at 4605 South Paulina Street on which to erect the clubhouse, and building operations are scheduled to start in the near future. It is planned to erect a two-story brick building, to be used solely by the club.

The plans for the building were drawn by Mr. Zaldokas, the famous Lithuanian architect. The first floor of the building will contain a bowling alley, billiard room, library and reading room, and a bathhouse. The second floor will contain a hall for meeting purposes and a gymnasium. The cost of erecting and equipping the building will be about ten thousand dollars.

The White Rose Club, which is a mutual aid society for Lithuanian men, was organized in 1908. It has a membership of over two hundred robust young men.

The officers of the Club are: J. Baciunas, president; A. V. Bagdonas, secretary; and A. Sobonkis, treasurer.

The White Rose Club will be the first Lithuanian organization in Chicago to have its own building.
