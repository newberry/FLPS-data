The meetings are held in the church hall at 18th Street and Union Ave.

The Society of Saint Anton of Padua was organized on June 30, 1900.

The Society consists of Lithuanians who are not inimical to the Roman Catholic faith.

This Society is a benefit one. The membership dues are twenty five cents per Month. To the sick members the society pays five dollars per week, when a member of the society dies every member pays one dollar for death benefits.

In the month of February, 1937, the Rosary Women's Society with seventy-five members joined this society. They brought a capital of $200.

In August, 1936, the St.John Evangelist society, with forty members and $1,000 capital also joined this society.

Now the society has about two hundred and fifty members and the capital is about $5,000.

Mrs. S. Eitmantiene, president, 5026 Washington Blvd.

A. Gricius, secretary, 1827 So. Union Ave.
