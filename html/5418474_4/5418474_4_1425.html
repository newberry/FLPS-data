The honorable Carl Schurz declared in Germany that the presidential election is not solely decided by graft money which is at the disposition of the fighting parties. Schurz is right! Money alone won't do it. There are assurances about the bestowal of jobs, too.

Hayes, for instance, would hardly have entered the White House, in spite of all election frauds and swindles, if he had not met one day with a tall elderly gentleman in Columbus, O., who later as secretary of the interior behaved very considerately towards the Northern Pacific Railway.
