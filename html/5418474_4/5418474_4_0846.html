The chief of police is a powerful man, but he is not powerful enough to catch Tascott or the murderer of Schuememann. Neither is he able to catch the man who killed the mailman, Kylfoile. But he still has enough power to refuse an amusement license to Battery D for an organization behind which a good half of the German population of Chicago stands.

The chief of police is slapping Germandom of the city in the face by refusing them something he has even granted to the organizers of "French Balls." We must admit, however, that the chief of police was acting under orders.
