Fifteen pocket-makers shops were completely organized by the district council of the Amalgamated Clothing Workers of America. President Sidney Hillman declared that the organizational work exceeded by far all expectations; Mr. Hillman is in charge of organizing the Chicago tailors.

The first large mass meeting in Chicago will be held Monday night, at Workmen's Hall, where Jacob Penkin of New York, Sidney Hillman, and others will address the assemblage.

President Hillman expects the hall to be filled to capacity with tailors who are looking forward to improving their conditions.
