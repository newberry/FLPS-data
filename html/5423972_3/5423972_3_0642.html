Chicago Cloakmakers will remain with all the Union conditions. There will be no reduction of wages in the cloak trade of Chicago and no standard of production.

The above was announced by Benjamin Schlesinger, President of the I.L.G.W. Union, after a conference which was held by the Chicago Cloakmakers and representatives of the Chicago Cloak Manufacturers Association.

Another demand that the Union won was that no employer is able to discharge a worker.

The representatives of the Union and of the Manufacturers Association agreed upon calling another conference in about five months from now, to consider the condition of the industry.

The American Federation of Labor extended congratulations to the I.L.G.W.U. for their victory.
