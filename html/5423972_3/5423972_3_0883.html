Harry Zef, former business agent of the joint board of the Cloakmakers, who was imprisoned as a result of the waist makers strike in 1917, was discharged and he returned to his family and his Union as a free man. After his acquittal he attended a meeting of local 100 Waist and Dressmakers Union, where he related his experiences in prison. The members tendered him a very cordial reception and presented him with a gold watch.
