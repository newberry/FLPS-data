The trust of the Jewish baker bosses, whose criminal acts were unveiled a few days ago by The World, was frightened by the wrath of public opinion, and they immediately reduced the prices of bread in Lawndale district, selling two and one-half pound loaves at 16¢ instead of 20¢.

Representatives of the Jewish unions in Chicago held a conference, Saturday evening, at which it was unanimously determined to start a powerful campaign through out the city in collecting a great fund for the World.

As it was understood, they will start collecting money in all trade shops, today, for a five thousand dollar fund, which is necessary in order to assure the success of our paper.
