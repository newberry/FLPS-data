The threatened strike of cloak, suit, and waist workers in Chicago has been averted. Judge Julian Mack, was selected chairman of a board of arbitration including among others, Samuel J. Kline, representing the Chicago cloak and suit manufacturers. It is planned to hold hearings in the City Council Chamber. Attorney A. J. Pflaum will present the employer's side of the situation.

The result of the arbitration will affect about 8,000 garment workers in one hundred and sixty five factories in Chicago. Negotiations for a peaceful settlement have been carried on for several days, largely through the intermediation of Judge Harry M. Fisher.
