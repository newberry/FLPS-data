The United Hebrew Trades held nomination and election of officers for the coming year, at a well attended meeting, Sunday morning.

The following delegates were elected: Balanik, of the carpenters Local 504, president; Rudin of the pressers, Local 18, vice-president; Hanock of the Shoe Repair Union, secretary-treasurer; M. Ziskind, of I. L. Peretz Writers Verlin, organizer, and Kazenovitz of operators, Local 5, sergeant-at-arms. Executive Board: Novak, Cohen, Trubakov, White, A. B. Green and Millstein.

Reports were given by delegates of the ladies tailors, shoe repairers, furriers and retail clerks. Delegate Green, of the retail clerks, reported that nineteen storekeepers from Maxwell street signed an agreement with the Retail Clerks Union granting all union conditions.

The United Hebrew Trades endorsed the Emergency Defense Conference to protect the imprisoned radicals and appointed two delegates to the conference - 2Holtzman and Ziskind. It was determined that at the next meeting the question of amalgamation should be discussed and what position the United Hebrew Trades shall take towards the movement. Delegate Rabinovitz, of Amalgamated Local 144, reported that his union contributed $200.00 to the Peoples Relief and $50.00 to the Los Angeles Sanitarium. The United Hebrew Trades appointed president Balanik and secretary Hanock as representatives to the Peretz Writers Verein's first anniversary banquet, October 14.

Upon requests of the painters Local 54, a banquet will be tendered in honor of Comrade Leon Hanock, secretary of the United Hebrew Trades, for his constant and devoted work to the United Hebrew Trades.

Upon the request of a delegate of the Waiters Union, it was determined to help organize the waiters in Jewish restaurants and lunch rooms and to help the ladies tailors organize the bushel-men in the cloak stores on Roosevelt Road.
