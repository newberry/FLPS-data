The tailors and pressers who are employed at second-hand tailoring and who are more exploited than others in their trade, have finally organized themselves into a Union.

The first organizational meeting was held yesterday in Arkin's Hall, 831 Maxwell street. About 100 workers were present. A committee was appointed to negotiate with the Amalgamated about uniting with the great tailoring body.

The following officers of the new Union were elected: Adelman, vice-president; Cohen, treasurer; Harry Cohen, recording secretary; Kramer, financial secretary.
