The first session of the second day of the Amalgamated Convention was taken up with reading of the report of the General Executive Board for the past two years.

One of the most important features in the report concerns the Unemployment Insurance Fund of Chicago which became effective May 1, 1924.

The benefits for the first six months ended October 1, 1924, amounting to $957,570.37.

Due to the larger expenses of the first season, the Trustees of the Fund determined that the second seasons benefits should be paid for in two weeks, in lieu of two and one-half weeks, in the first three seasons amounted to $1,990,888.15.
