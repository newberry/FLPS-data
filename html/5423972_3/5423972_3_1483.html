A left wing group, influenced by bolshevism, had grown upon the branches, which had organized themselves into a caucus, this caucus decided a plan of action which bound its membership, who attempted to carry out these plans at the regular meeting.

The right wing which controlled the City Central Committee, attempted to offset this, by proposing a resolution that no member could adhere to any other political organization (meaning the caucus). This resolution was voted down whereupon the City Central used its right to proclaim this as a basic principle. This caused a split - the socalled lefters leaving the Socialist Party and organizing a "left wing."

The split is termed reorganization' that is, only members of the right wing remained. Sympathy to Russia is still maintained.
