The Jewish bakers of Chicago ended their strike last night when their employers came to an agreement with the Jewish Baker's Union.

It was decided by the committee of the employers to accept the terms of the Bakers Union and settle the strike.

The strike lasted three and a half weeks. The' baker bosses" had decided to combat the union on all fronts. They demanded the union to reduce six dollars a week from the prevailing wages, no pay for holidays, no extra pay for night work, and to be at liberty in hiring extra helpers.

The union rejected all these demands and withdrew the demand of a five dollar raise in order to keep the bosses from complaining to the public that they are paying the union bakers too high wages.
