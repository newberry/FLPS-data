It was announced yesterday from the [Dressmakers] Union's headquarters that eighty-one of the biggest shops have already settled with the Union, agreeing to its demands.

Judge Sullivan's injunction hinders the Union, particularly the pickets. The strike leaders state, however, that even the bosses have begun to realize that one cannot manufacture dresses with an injunction. Good workers are needed for that, but the good workers are striking now.

The strike leaders were very optimistic yesterday about the situation, though they admitted that the fight still rages bitterly.
