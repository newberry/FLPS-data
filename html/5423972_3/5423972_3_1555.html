The J. S. P. Association is an organization that is established on the principles of the International Social Democracy, and in order to propagate the philosophy of Socialism, it has established a daily Jewish Socialist periodical and other writings, which the Association will, from time to time, publish. The Association is obliged to agitate for the Socialist Party in America, which represents the International Social Democracy. And being that the Socialist Party of America (S. P.) is the party that represents these principles, the Association supports the S. P. of America. The Association is obligated to support, agitate, defend and work for the trade unions, Workmen's Circle, cooperative and progressive organizations, which are recognized by the Jewish Socialist Publishing Association.

Constitution. Article I. Membership.

Members shall consist of delegates from organizations. (a). Delegates from organizations. Every labor organization that is recognized by the \2Jewish Socialist Publishing Association, may belong to the Association and is entitled to two delegates. (b). Individual Members. Every Jewish-speaking Socialist, who is not younger than twenty years, who agrees to the principles declared by the Association, and who lives up to the following clauses, may become a member of the J. P. S.: 1. The candidate must be a good-standing member of the Socialist Party, who is recognized by the Association not less than six months. 2. If the candidate is working at a trade where a union exists, which is recognized by the Association, he must be a good-standing member of that union.

2. The application must be read at a regular meeting of the Association. The chairman may ask of any member if there are any charges against the candidate. The application shall then be submitted to the Board of Candidates, together with the objections, if any.

3. The secretary of the Board of Candidates must inquire, in writing, of the secretary of the organizations (where the candidate is a member) if they have any objections against him.

4. The committee reports to the next delegate meeting and if the report is favorable, the candidate becomes accepted, when a majority of votes are given to the candidate. If the report is favorable, the candidate must receive three-quarters of the vote.

Article 3. Duties and privileges of the members.

1. Every member of the Association must pay twenty-five cents initiation fee and one dollar annually for contributions, twenty-five cents every three months.

2. If a member owes for two quarterly dues, he shall be notified that he is suspended, and is given a period of three months to be reinstated, and if he fails to pay at the end of three months, he shall be dropped as member, from the Association.

3. A suspended member loses all privileges as a member of the J. S. P. Association, until he becomes of good-standing.

4. If a member submits reasons and asks that they should wait for his dues, and if the reasons are satisfactory to the Association, the Association can determine to wait for the dues.

Privileges of members. 1. Every good-standing member and every delegate to the Association is privileged to participate in all matters that appear before the Association.

Article 4. Meetings.

1. The Association shall hold regular meetings every second and third Thursday of the month, with the exception of the general meetings as further explained.

2. Twenty members are a quorum to open a regular meeting.

3. The Association shall hold four general meetings annually. The fourth Thursday of January. The fourth Thursday of April. The fourth Thursday of July. The fourth Thursday of October.

4. A special meeting of the Association can be called: (a). If it is decided upon at a regular meeting. (b). If it is decided upon by the executive committee. (c). If ten good-standing members of the Association demand it by mail.

5. The secretary shall notify all meetings in at least three days before the meetings.

6. If a member is not present at four or more meetings, he is not permitted \6to vote at the first meeting he attends.

7. All business shall be transacted, at the regular meeting, in the following manner: a. Vote for a chairman of the meeting. b. Reading the minutes. c. Reading the names of the new candidates. d. Report of Board of Candidates. h. Induction of new members. i. Correspondence. j. Payment of dues. k. Executive committee's report. k. Unfinished business. k. New business.

8. Donations or loans to any one exceeding the sum of $25 shall not be made at a regular meeting; a special meeting must be called for the purpose.

9. The following agenda shall be transacted at a general meeting: a. Reading of minutes. b. Payment of dues. c. Executive committee's report. d. Quarter-annually financial report of the Association. e. Vote for members of the executive committee. f. Committee report. g. Amendments to the constitution and by-laws. h. Unfinished business. i. New business. j. General discussion.

\7

10. Thirty good-standing members constitute a quorum to open a general meeting.

11. A special meeting shall only transact matters to which these meetings were specially called.

12. Resolutions that are made later than 12 o'clock midnight are not official and shall not be recognized.

13. If a matter is extended until 12 o'clock, the decision must be postponed until the next regular or special meeting, if the meeting determines to call such before 12 o'clock.

Article 5. Officials.

1. The officers of the Association consist of: President, vice-president, financial secretary, treasurer, recording and corresponding secretary. Executive committee consists of: President, secretary, treasurer and seven appointed members. Sergeant-at-arms.

\8

Article 6. Priveleges and duties of the officers. President.

1. The president is elected for six months; in January and in July. The second Thursday in January (at a regular meeting) nominations for president are held.

2. One president can only be elected (a) who is a member of the party for twelve years.

3. The president shall sign all official papers as: notes, checks, etc.

4. The president shall give security, the sum that is determined at the last general meeting of the calendar year.

Article 7. Right and duties of the vice-president.

1. One can only be elected to the office of vice-president, who possesses the qualifications.

2. The vice-president shall be elected for six months.

3. If the president is impeached, resigns, or dies, the vice-president shall fulfill his office, until a new president is elected.

\9

4. The vice-president has the same privileges and duties as the president, when he occupies the president's office.

Article 8. Rights and duties of the secretary, treasurer.

1. One can only be elected to the offices of the secretary and treasurer, who possesses the qualifications which are posted in the presidential election. He is elected for one year.

2. The secretary-treasurer shall sign all official papers.

3. The secretary-treasurer shall give security, of which it was decided at the last regular meeting of the calendar year.

4. The secretary-treasurer shall pay all bills, that are agreed upon at a meeting and to keep record of all incomes and expenses.
