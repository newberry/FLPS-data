The popular society opened the season with a successful social last Friday. As guests of honor were invited opera singer Rolf Hammer and painist Ludwig Haugan. At the last moment Mr. Haugan was prevented from coming. Mr. Hammer rendered several numbers which were received with vigorous applause and enthusiasm.

The president, Guttorm Viker, thanked Mr. Hammer, not merely for the songs of the evening, but for the great and valuable work he is doing by his song, creating longing for the old home, directing many a thought to Norway.

Mr. Hammer responded to the speech with a song. Dr. Warloe spoke warmly for the authot of the club-song, Olaf Schroeder, who is in Norway.

Editor Lange spoke for the club and its new hall and home. Bastian Horn spoke for the only Swede present, Architect Carl Sava, who in return brought warm greeting from Norway, which country he had visited only three weeks ago, 2 being the one present who most recently set his foot on Norwegian soil. He offered a toast for a new North of the future. The speech was received with enthusiasm and was followed by a solo by Hammer, "Du gamla, de friska" etc., the audience rising. A brief speech for Norway by Bastian Horn, and "Ja vi elsker" was sung by Mr. John Lund. The club - paper "Bladlusen" was read and vigorously applauded. The social was the first of the season for the Norwegian club, and a grand success.

The officers of the club are as follows:

President, Guttorm Viker;

Vice Pres., John Batrer;

Secretary, Albert Mohn;

Treasurer, Olaf Balstad;

Librarian, Sofus Togens.
