At its last meeting the Norwegian National Association passed a resolution censuring member bodies which hold private festivals on occasions when the Association is putting on a city-wide celebration, such as May 17 and Leif Erikson Day. The resolution was due to the action of The Chicago Norwegian Club, which not only held a private festival on May 17 but invited the League's speakers to attend. It is the object of the Norwegian National Association to promote harmony and co-operation between all Norwegian Americans of Chicago and vicinity. Arranging competitive banquets and programs is anything but conducive to harmony.

The Association is now fully in harness for raising funds for the gift to Norway in 1914, and its committee is headed by L. C. Grundeland. The gift will be a fund to be used as the Norwegian authorities think best.
