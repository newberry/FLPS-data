After printing an article about the restaurant business last week, we cannot possibly let the matter drop now. Many objected to the truth that coffee, rolls and butter should be sold for more than five cents. In Chicago there are over 900 Greek restaurants. An average of 200 customers enter each restaurant daily. Two hundred nickels a day amount to 6,000 in a month and 75,000 in a year--equivalent to $3,750. Nine hundred restaruants ought to make $3,375,000 annually. This would be a very good income for the restaurateurs, if--.

The Greek restaurateurs do not know how to systemize their work. There is much waste and needless expense because of this. By uniting and finding means of cutting the overhead, more profit will be saved for the restaurateur.
