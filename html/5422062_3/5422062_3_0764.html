p. 2.- The first Commission appointed by a foreign government, to "A Century of Progress," met last Friday with the Exposition officials in the Administration Building.

The Greek government's commission consists of the Hon. Geo. Depastas, Consul-General for Greece as chairman, and the following Chicagoans of Greek descent: Dr. Nicholas Salopoulos, Editor of Saloniki; John D. Dritsas, Deputy Greek Consul; Andrew Karzas; Anthony Polemis, and Paul Demos, Chairman of the Greek section of the Exposition's Foreign Nationalities Committee.

The Commissioners were guests of the Executive Committee at a luncheon in the trustees' room, and then were taken on an inspection tour.

Greece has already officially accepted the invitation issued by President Hoover to participate in the 1933 Exposition. The function of the Commission will be to report to the Greek Government as to what form the Greek participation should take.
