Last Sunday, the Kastritans of Tania gave their dance, the proceeds of which were to go towards a statue of T. Kolokotronis to be erected in the town of his birth.

People began coming early and by 8 o'clock all halls were filled. Some were dancing, others were eating and drinking and still others were conversing with friends and relatives.

Speeches lasted about twenty minutes. Lawyer P. Limperis spoke about Theodore Kolokotrinis, he was followed by Dr. Sacopoulos, A.A. Pantelis, Dr. Theodore and S, Kotakis. Greek and American dancing followed the speeches.
