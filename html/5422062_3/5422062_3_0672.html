The play, "The Heroism of Macedonia," given by the dramatic group of the Messinian Brotherhood last Sunday, was a tremendous success. Despite the fact that none of the leading actors had ever stepped upon a stage before, the acting was of excellent quality.

This is a fine example of what a Greek Club can do if it applies itself to the betterment of itself and the community. A common purpose and program for the various clubs of Chicago would help to unify them and enable them, in turn, to help the community. Many problems, such as Greek education of children, could be solved by a little cooperation between organizations.
