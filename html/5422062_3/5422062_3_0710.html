The Picnic of Stereo-Elladition

The outdoor entertainment of Stereo-Elladition, of last year, in Polonia Grove, drew a large crowd as it did this year despite continuous rain.

There were not only Stereo-Elladites out for the celebration but other fellow-nationals, who honored them by their presence and who know the value of a Roumeliotiko party.

Barbecue lambs, and the famous Kokoretsi of Roumeli, being roasted on the spot, was being supplied, abundantly, and the merriment was getting more and more lively considering the fact that selected wines were accompanying the barbecue.

The Parking Shed was filled to capacity, and Greek dances by robust young men and charming women were going on with rhythme and grace.

Young men dressed with Foustanellas and girls in the national costumes were rendering the dances picturesquely that we felt for a moment we were in a Greek fair of Roumeli because the Roumeliotes dance the Greek dances with special grace and dexterity.

The president of the Society, McGikes, lawyer, with Messrs. G. Papadopoulos and Athan. Patrinos composed the reception committee, and Mr. Bezaitis directed the dance in accordance with the splendid orchestra of P.A.T.S.P. brothers. Costas Mouyas and George Zavertis led the dance, dressed in Foustennelas and the ladies dressed in Greek costumes, following.
