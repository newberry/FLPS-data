On February 16, a benefit program was given in behalf of the Embros [Greek progressive, even radical, newspaper], at the West End Women's Club, 37 South Ashland Avenue.

The first number, a gypsy dance, was followed by Russian and Greek classical dances, all performed by students of Mr. Nicholas Tsoukalas. The main part of the program was a play having social significance, which began at 7 P. M. It was "The Country of the Poor," written by the Italian...This drama portrayed the tragic events in the life of an immigrant who came to America because he could not earn enough in the old country to feed his family.

After the play refreshments were served and dancing took place.
