The Greek Community of our city acquitted itself admirably last Sunday at the reception given for Spyros Matsoukas. We had been waiting for him a long time, and, when he finally came, our enthusiasm was unquenchable.

More elated were we when we heard that he has sent, in his 18 months, here, over $103,000 home to Greece. He raised $103,000 from the Greeks of America and he sent $103,000 to Greece. He is working, without any personal gain to himself, for the cause of Greece.

The Greek press in America has dubbed him a thief, a liar, a usurper, etc., but if this were so, would he send $103,000 of the $103,000 to Greece? Wouldn't he keep a goodly portion for himself? Would he be working so hard among so many obstacles if he weren't the genuine thing?

That is only one mistake of the Greek press. Among our Greek newspapers the Atlantis collects $70,000 annually from subscribers, the Pan Hellenic collects $40,000, the Athena $5,000, and Immigrant $2,500, making a total of $117,500 a year or $235,000 in two years' time.

Greeks of America, we are throwing $235,000 away every two years to worthless newspapers. Of that $235,000, how much was sent to Greece? How much was used to improve the Greeks of America? How much was used to preserve Hellenism? None!

The press says that it does much good for the Greeks of America and should be paid accordingly. What has the press done?

1. Has it cooperated to solve the immigration problem?

2. Has it shown the young people how to become systemized in their occupations?

3. Has it ever helped the church?

4. Has it been used to find employment for our countrymen?

5. Has it ever tried to build a hospital for the ignorant Greeks who cannot speak English?

6. Has it explained the Greek religion to its readers so the common people can understand it?

7. Has it told the 3,000 Greek merchants how to unite and become more progressive?

8. Has it said anything about church officials who rob the poor of their last cent by promising them a holy blessing?

9. In a community of 25,000 Greeks, has it tried to establish a gymnasium, or club rooms, or a school for the younger people?

10. Has it tried to explain American politics and the duty of voters to the uneducated Greeks?

11. Has it told them what patriotism and duty is?

12. Has it tried to naturalize them?

These are only twelve mistakes of the Greek press in America. $235,000 bi-annually is a lot to pay to people who make such costly mistakes. Wake up, countrymen!
