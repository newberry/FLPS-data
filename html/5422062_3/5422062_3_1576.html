As athletes, Americans are first class Greeks. This was disclosed by Professor John A. Scott, professor of Greek, in the Northwestern University.

"No nation resembles the ancient Greeks in attitude more than the Americans," writes Professor Scott. "No nation has created anything superior in the field of athletics to the Greeks."

The enthusiastic devotion of a nation to athletics is the greatest contribution towards democratic ideas, honesty, temperance, straight-forwardness, and preservation of manly instinct.

Athletics was to the ancient Greeks, and is to Americans, the biggest contribution to the health of the race. We must remain true to athletics \2and stop commercializing them because commercializing sports is the greatest drawback to their development.

Professor John A. Scott is the brother of the president of Northwestern University, Walter Dill Scott.
