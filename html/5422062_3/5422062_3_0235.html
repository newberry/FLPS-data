p. 2.- Art. 1. The Greek restaurateurs of Chicago, Illinois, United States of America, have met together on March 1, 1910 and organized the Greek Restaurant Association of Chicago, for the better development of restaurants.

Art. 2. All Greek restaurateurs are elegible for membership.

Art. 3. Before becoming a member the restaurateur must fill out an application and have it signed by the secretary of the club.

Art. 4. He must take an oath of allegiance to the organization.

Art. 5. Members will enjoy all privileges granted by the organization unless otherwise specified.

Art. 6. The Association will take care of all grievances and complaints.

Art. 7. The Association will lend money to borrowers under specific rules and regulations.

Art. 8. The committee will work at all times for the benefit of all. They welcome any criticisms or suggestions at all times.

Art. 9. No business dealings can be done with a restaurateur of another city or another nationality without the consent of the association.

Art. 10. The committee will consist of 18 members: 1 president, 1 vice-president, 1 treasurer, 1 assistant treasurer, 1 secretary, 1 recording secretary, and 12 committeemen. These will constitute a board of directors.

Art. 12. Vice-president takes the place of the president upon his absence.
