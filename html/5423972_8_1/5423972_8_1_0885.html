June 10, 1914. Rep. Adolph J. Sabath (Ill.) introduces bill containing amendments proposed in Burnett-Dillingham bill, but omitting literacy test and other restrictive features. Bill provides for representation of aliens by counsel or other adviser at hearings before boards of special inquiry; for a rehearing after first rejection; the appointment of members on boards of inquiry by Commissioner of Labor. Bill omits provision that hearings shall be "separate and apart from the public."
