The University of Chicago is now accepting registrants for fourteen courses being given during the coming year in Hebrew and Jewish fields, - Contemporary Jewish Problems; Jewish Folkways and Institutions; Intermediate and Elementary Hebrew; the Jewish Diaspora; Hebrew and Jewish History; Biblical Literature; Methods of Teaching in Jewish Religious Schools, and Problems in American Jewish Education, among other subjects.

These courses are being given at the University College in the Lake View Building and also on the campus. The professors presenting them are all members of the faculty of the College of Jewish Studies. Doctors Alexander Dushkin, Isadore Keyfitz and Abraham J. Levy and S. Golub. This reflects high credit upon the Board of Jewish Education, which has chosen for Chicago the best teachers obtainable. The Autumn Quarter at the University begins October 1.
