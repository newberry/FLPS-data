The Clay Literary Association was established in Chicago by eleven Jewish young men in September, 1859. The object of the Association was to gain a more intimate union among the young men of Chicago and for the promotion of literature.

The officers were: Henry N. Hart, President; D.J. Boehm, Vice-President, G.A. Levi, Recording Secretary; Martin Barbe, Financial Secretary; and F.S. Mandle, Treasurer.
