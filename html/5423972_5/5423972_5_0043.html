As is known to the readers of the Courier, two pictures in Mr. Block's recent exhibit at Chicago's Arts Club were interesting and impressive portraits of Jewish types: "The Writer," and "The Psalm Singer."

Besides two non-Jewish art connoisseurs, there were many amateur judges present. Of his masterpieces, the Jewish painter Block said, "that he hoped they might land in Jewish hands."

Last week Mr. S. B. Komaiko imspected the two pictures and bought them.

We hear that Mr. Komaiko also wishes to obtain a third picture of Mr. Block's known under the title of "The Soul of a Violin," or "The Transmigration of a Melody."
