Yesterday, at 3 P. M., the 32nd annual graduation exercises were held at the Jewish Training School, 12th and Clinton Streets.

The ceremony and examinations were carried through splendidly. The girls have distinguished themselves in the art of dressmaking.

The boys exhibited their carpentry. Several made beautiful articles and after it passed inspection it was given to them as gifts.

Mr. J. L. Seck, superintendent, was highly pleased with the good work the children have accomplished in the course of the past year. He also expressed himself for an increase in education.

\2

"Any child is welcome in our school," he said. "If the school can in any way help the child, it will do so in both a scholistic and a material sense, i. e., children will be taught trades, for which they are best adapted."
