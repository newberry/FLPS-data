The Chicago Hebrew Institute will now have as its superintendent, one of the most capable Russian-Jewish men in America, Mr. Philip Seaman, former superintendent of the St. Louis Educational Alliance, and now associated with the New York Removal Office, (Immigrant Society).

The committee, confronted with the task of selecting a suitable man for this important office, announced yesterday that, after a great deal of correspondence, Mr. Seaman finally has agreed to accept the position.

Mr. Seaman has been for many years connected with charity work. As superintendent of the St. Louis Educational Alliance, he was liked by all classes of Jews.

Mr. Seaman departed from the St. Louis Educational Alliance because his energy requires a larger field for activity.
