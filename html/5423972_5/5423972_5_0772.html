Of the research work of the Jewish Historical Society there seems to be no end. The staff of the Society has been busy going over the original records, many of which have not seen the light of day for seventy-five years.

Thousands of questionnaires have been mailed out to men and women of Chicago, who have helped to make history. The results have been more than gratifying, and after a complete check of all the material, the book will be ready for press.

Those who have been privileged to see the manuscript and the illustrations are unanimous in asserting that not only will it be an inspiration for the present generation, but will be a guiding light for many generations to come.
