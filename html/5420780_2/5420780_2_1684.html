At a meeting of the Society for the Building of a Danish Old People's Home on June 10, 1914, it was decided to build, and to accept the plans approved by the building committee, A. M. Petersen, Architect B. Winslow, Lauritz Olesen, Wm. Mogensen, and Architect John C. Christensen. It is to be a two-story building with twenty-four rooms. The front of the new building will measure 116 feet, and is to be connected with the old building by a corridor. The cost will be $20,000, to which has to be added $4,500 for red tiles for the roof.
