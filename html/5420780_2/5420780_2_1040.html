The Chicago Public Library has 211,000 volumes, out of which 4,664 volumes are in the Scandinavian language. In the periodical room there are forty-three papers and periodicals published in the United States or in Denmark.

Fred. W. Hild, the librarian, states that the Danes of Chicago are among the foremost readers in town. They read the most books.

The Danish Consul, Otto Dreier, of Chicago, has done much to help the Danes get books in their own language for the library.
