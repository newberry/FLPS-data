The Scandinavian-American Medical Society of Chicago observed the twenty-fifth anniversary of its founding last August, and has now, in memory of that occasion, published a brief outline of the history of the Society, under the title Silver Jubilee Souvenir.

The book is written by the popular Dr. B. Meyer, a charter member of the Society. Dr. S. Jacobsen, Dane, was the first president of the Society. Later he went to Denmark where he died in 1895.

The author honors the memory of the famous Danish surgeon Dr. Christian Fenger who in 1900, two years before his death, became an honorary member of the Society.

Among the other Danish doctors, who have been active in the Society, is Dr. Svenning Dahl, who in 1909 held the office of president.
