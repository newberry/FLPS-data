The Danish parade started from the corner of Milwaukee and Indiana Avenues. Mr. Ockenholdt was the chief marshal. The Danes marched downtown and took the Illinois Central train to the Fair. When the crowd passed the Danish Hall everybody started to sing the Danish national hymn, "King Christian stood by the lofty mast".

At the festival hall Mr. C. Nichelsen, the chairman of the Danish committee, made a speech of welcome. Mr. [William Jennings] Bryan spoke in praise of Denmark. He stated that the inscription over the Danish Hall, "Pax et Labor" (Peace and Work), was eminently true of the little country. The Consul General, Erik Meyer, spoke for the King of Denmark and the President of the United States.
