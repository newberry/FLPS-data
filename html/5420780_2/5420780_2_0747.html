The Danish Workers' Singing Society celebrated the 18th anniversary of its founding last Saturday with a spirited celebration in its castle on the corner of Albany and Armitage Avenues, which was festively decorated with flags and flowers and banners in all the colors of the rainbow.

Delegations from the two other large singing societies Harmonien and Magneten were present with greetings and best wishes to their colleagues.

At the table, the traditional speeches for America, Denmark, and the ladies were delivered and many others besides.

The best talent of the three societies entertained generously with songs and recitations, and a genuine singer spirit and comradeship prevailed from beginning to end.
