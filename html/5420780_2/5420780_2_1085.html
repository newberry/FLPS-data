On Easter morning the Danish colony awoke to a city-wide excitement; the English-language papers carried big headlines clear across the front page:

"Duel between Christian Botker, editor of Revyen (The Review), and Edward Petersen, editor of Chicago Posten (The Chicago Post)".

Soon it was rumored that the duelists, the seconds, and the doctors (one always has a doctor or two at a duel) had been arrested and [would be] indicted.

The seconds were Anton Kvist and the singer Andreas Frandsen for Botker and Thorvald Sommer and Edward Praem for Peterson.
