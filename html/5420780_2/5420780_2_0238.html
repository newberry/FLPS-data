Peter B. Nelson, prominent Chicago attorney, was born in Denmark. His father, one of the leaders of the Society Dania of Racine, was for a number of years supreme secretary of the United Danish Societies of America. Peter B. Nelson was educated in the public schools of Racine and was admitted to the bar while in that city. He was elected mayor of Racine. He was also district attorney for Racine county and president of Wisconsin League of Municipalities.

In 1915 he came to Chicago and began the practice of law, and has built up a large clientele in this city. He is the president of the American-Denmark committee of the World's Fair.
