Hejmdal, a Danish singing society on the South Side which has been in existence for eight years, gave an excellent concert on October 20 in Douglas Hall. The director is Professor Petersen. The best part on the program was the "Soldiers' Chorus" from "Faust". Miss Ingeborg Sorensen gave some well-played piano solos. Another soloist was Mr. Arthur Froembling, baritone. Mr. M. Pedersen gave an excellent cornet solo. Mr. Andrew Nielsen gave a couple of humorous readings.

The committee for the arrangement of this concert consisted of Carl W. Larsen, Hans Jespersen, Fred Jensen, Albert Nielsen, and Harry Schnorr.
