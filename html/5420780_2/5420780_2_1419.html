p.5......."Sons of Denmark and " Daughters of Denmark" celebrated heir 31st birthday with a very jolly and successful festival at Dania Hall last Sunday. The hall was beautifully decorated with flags and flowers and the gaily set made "Birthday guests" feel in a festive mood.

The committee chairman, Mr. P.S.S. Baile, in a few appropriate words extended a cordial welcome to all. Then he bestowed the honor acting as toastmaster to Mr. Soren Petersen, who very able acquitted himself of the task.

After appetites had been appeased, Mrs. Elvine Jorgensen was called upon. She spoke on the society "Daughters' of Denmark," and gave resume of activities and purpose of the "Society."

\2

Mr. Lindberg attended the program of the "Sons of Denmark". He strongly urged all Danes to join the Society and to work for a great membership.

The speaker called attention to the"Society's" first President, Mr. Josephsen, whom they were pleased to have in their midst this evening, also another member, a, Mr. Friis.

Mrs. Cecilie Petersen sang one of her own compositions written for the occasion. It contained little sarcasm directed both to the committee as well as the management.

The next speaker was our well known and eloquent Mr. Geo. P. Jensen, who made a sincere and patriotic speech for America, which, was received with a storm of applause.

After singing "My Country tis of thee" Mr. Christ Jorgensen spoke warmly for Denmark, and although he had not been there for over thirty years, he still loved his mother country.

\3

Mr. Magnus Sorensen spoke in behalf of the ladies. A song was sung in their honor, Then the President of "Sons of Denmark Society" spoke. Mr. Josephsen gave a short resume of what the "Society" had accomplished. He spoke in glowing terms of Dr. Max Henius and Dr. P. Clemmensen.

Mrs. Lindberg recalled in her speech the unselfish work Mrs. Heilmuth had performed for the "Daughters of Denmark", she was also certain that Mrs. Heilmuth's thoughts were with then this evening, and it was moved and seconded to send Mr. and Mrs. Heilmuth a greeting by telegram.

A telegram was reserved from Dr. Henius and read by the master of ceremonies, and which was received with thundering applause.

Mr. Baile thanked the young ladies, who had served at the table as well as all others, who had helped to make the evening a successful one. It was an event, which everybody could be proud of having attended.
