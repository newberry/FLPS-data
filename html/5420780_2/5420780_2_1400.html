At the meeting of delegates from the different Danish societies in Chicago on January 16, in Schoenhofen's Hall, a vote was taken as to where the statue of Hans Christian Andersen should be placed. Humboldt Park received thirteen votes and Jackson Park twelve votes. The placement committee was present with the exception of Henry L. Hertz, who was ill. There were Captain Larsen, Clerk Salmonsen, the Reverend Dan and Consul Nielsen, of Racine. No result came of the meeting.
