Walhalla, a Danish society on the South Side, provides life insurance ($500) and help in sickness. It was founded March 17, 1883. There were 518 members on July 1, 1913. Walhalla owns its building, worth $35,000. It meets every Tuesday. The secretary is R. Egebergh, 24 East Adams Street. During the year the society took in $3,840.95 and spent $3,567.06. It paid out $3,344.19 to sick members. At festivals the society took in $526.74 and spent $353.76.
