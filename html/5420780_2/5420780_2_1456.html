The Danish Sisterhood (Det Danske Sostersamfund, or D.S.S.) was founded on December 15, 1885, in Negaumee, Michigan, by Mrs. Christine Hemmingsen. Members of the Danish Brotherhood could be taken in but without the right to vote. Niels Hemmingsen was the first Supreme President. The first convention was held in Burlington, Iowa, in 1887, and the second in Chicago in 1890, where the society was incorporated. The Sisterhood now has 125 lodges with 6,287 members. All women born in Denmark or in America of Danish parents, and all women married to Danish husbands can be members if between the ages of eighteen and fifty.
