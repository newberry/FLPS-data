When some time ago, Mr. William D. Haywood was elected a member of the Socialist Party's Executive Committee Revyen objected, and pointed out how great a mistake it was to entrust a man of Mr. Haywood's caliber with so much responsibility.

Gradually this point of view has penetrated to a great many circles. It is now dominating public opinion and the issue will be put to a vote to decide if Mr. Haywood should be removed from office and expelled from the party because of his anarchistic and syndicalistic theories.
