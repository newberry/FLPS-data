William Hansen, who has a cigar and confectionery establishment at No. 405 Division Street, Chicago, is one of the oldest business men in that street.

He was born February 7, 1846, in Flensburg, then a part of Denmark, now a part of Germany. He is a ship carpenter by trade and has in this capacity sailed the seven seas for twelve years. In 1872 he bought his own ship and sailed as captain for five years.

He came to America and Chicago in 1873. He worked for some years in a box and barrel factory and as a shipbuilder. In 1884 he opened a confectionery and tobacco store on Division Street.

In 1873 he was married in Denmark to Miss Anna Larsen. Mr. Hansen is a member of Covenant Lodge No. 526, Ancient Free and Accepted Masons; Wicker Park Lodge No. 281, Independent Order of Odd Fellows; and Victory Encampment No. 108, of the same order. He is a Republican.
