During their automobile ride along North Avenue next Monday afternoon, the visiting Danish student singers will notice the Danish Corydon store, a department store with a great future, located on the next corner from Humboldt Park.

In the course of a few years Mr. Corydon has doubled his sales, and is now employing thirty persons, among whom are fifteen Danish clerks. Together with a few other store owners, he maintains an office on 14th Street in New York City in order to quickly supplement his stock with the latest products on the world-market.

A pleasant and prosperous store, the policy of which it to treat its customers in such a manner that they like to come back.
