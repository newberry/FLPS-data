It is with "mixed" feelings one reads through these 191 pages of Mixed Memoirs From a 40-Year Sojourn in Chicago, and one lays them aside with -well, disgust is the best word for it. A more suitable title for the book would be "Loose Talk."

The book is chiefly filled with "rumors," mostly rumors of the more unsavory kind, but sprinkled in among all these non-essential topics, one finds a few interesting memories of some important persons and events of the period, and quite a number of people receive favorable mention.

The main objection to the book is that it does not give a true picture of the Danish colony in Chicago during the period in question, and in \2fact, this may not have been the purpose of the author; it may sell better the way it is.

Another objection is that it gives no account of events and conditions of the last 15-20 years.

The memoirs are certainly "mixed," if this word is to signify a mess of strongly colored stories and lack of coordination and unity.

It is difficult to find an acceptable excuse for the publishing of these "mixed memories."

(Signed) C.H.W. Hasselriis
