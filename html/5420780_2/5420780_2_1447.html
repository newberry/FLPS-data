Lodge No. 18 of the Danish Brotherhood observed the 30th anniversary of its founding with an animated celebration last Sunday in Dania Hall.

The celebrants numbered about 250, including members, delegations from other Chicago lodges, and a few specially invited guests.

The table was set in the big dining room, and during the festive meal the customary speeches were delivered, including a review of the history of the lodge.

Then followed the lighter part of the program, which consisted of cabaret entertainment in the appropriately decorated basement, and dancing in the ball room.

The arrangement committee deserves credit for its excellent work.
