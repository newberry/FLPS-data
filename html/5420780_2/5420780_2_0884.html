Ibsen's "Ghosts" was presented in Bjorgvin's Hall last Sunday, and the performance was a decided success, which has hardly been equaled in Chicago's Danish-Norwegian theatre.

Mrs. Borgny Hammer, as director and instructor deserves most of the credit for the excellent acting and the tasteful stage settings.

She herself played the role of "Mrs. Alving" with artistic strength and feeling, and Mr. Emil Midelfart, as "Oswald," gave a memorable performance. They both reached the artistic climax in the stirring scenes of the third act.

The other roles were also in good hands. The audience responded with thundering applause, and the actors took the bow several times.

The performance will be repeated on May 4 in Bjorgin's Hall.
