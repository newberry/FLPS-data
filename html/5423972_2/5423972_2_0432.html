Mr. Lucius L. Solomons, first vice president of the Independent Order B'nai B'rith, addressed members of the Order in Chicago at the Hotel La Salle commemorating the eighty-fifth anniversary of the organization. In his address Mr. Solomons stated that Jews are regarded as inferior social beings because the Jew allows the critical world to judge him as a member of a race instead of as an individual.

"Under pressure of criticism," said Mr. Solomons, "the Jew has suffered, and doubly, because he has allowed himself to be judged as a class, not as an individual."

Mr. Solomons denounced Jewish authors, stage and motion picture producers who presented undesirables of their own race to the contempt of the Jews and the world. "The world unfortunately judges a race or class by its worst members," said Mr. Solomons.
