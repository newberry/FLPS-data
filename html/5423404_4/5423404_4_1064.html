We reported some time ago that the Chicago branch of the Society for the Preservation of Swedish Culture in Foreign Countries had suggested to headquarters in Sweden that the organization's 1933 World Convention be held in Chicago in connection with the great exposition which will be held here that year.

A. G. Witting, president of the local society, has just received the following reply,dated November 24, "Convention in Chicago in 1933 unanimously decided on".

(Signed) H. Jorth

Preliminary work has already started in preparation for the anticipated great influx of Swedish visitors during the exposition year, and for Swedish participation in the great fair.
