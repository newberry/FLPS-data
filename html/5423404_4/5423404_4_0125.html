This institution is the Home for the Aged operated by the Swedish Methodists of America, and is located at 5015 North Paulina Street. Its 1909 yearbook has just been published, and Reverend John Bendix, the financial agent, reports that the Home now is caring for thirty-five residents, of which the oldest, Mrs. Anna Ulfeldt of Chicago, has just observed her eighty-ninth birthday. Four new guests were admitted during the past year.

Alfred Anderson is chairman of the board of directors of the Home, and Mr. and Mrs. Gustdahl are in charge.

Last year the Home had an income of $7,944.36, and as of January 1, 1910, cash on hand amounted to $221.40.
