The Swedish Engineers Society held its third annual meeting with a banquet last Saturday at the Boston Oyster House. Many prominent persons were present, even from Milwaukee, Wis., and Columbus, Ohio.

The report was presented. The society has now 168 members. There is $800 in the treasury. The retiring president, G. A. M. Liljencrantz, was elected an honorary member. The newly elected president, Dr. Ernst J. Berg, professor at the electrical department at the University of Illinois, Urbana, Ill., delivered a very interesting address. His topic was: "The successful engineer." Attorney G. Bernhard Anderson spoke about "The old and the new homeland." The Swedish National Anthem "Du gamla du fria," and "America" were sung in unison.
