A second meeting was held yesterday at 4 P. M. at the Polish Hall, Bradley and Noble Streets, to help the destitute Poles of Nebraska. Many leading Poles of Chicago were present. Reverend Vincent Barzynski, pastor of St. Stanislaus Kostka Parish, opened the meeting and presided as chairman. F. Golupski acted as recording secretary.

An account of the conditions facing the Poles in Nebraska was presented by Peter Kiolbassa. When he concluded, a collection was made. [List of donors omitted in translation.]

Mr. Kiolbassa announced that contributions up to the present time have exceeded two hundred and thirty-six dollars, and that the money will be sent to the needy Poles.

Boleslaus Klarkowski announced to the audience that a special meeting of Polish businessmen will be held in the near future. The date of the meeting will be announced later.
