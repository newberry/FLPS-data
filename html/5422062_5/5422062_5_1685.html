P.4--When the Argileonis, the Spartan mother of Brasidas, heard about the death of her son in Amphipolis, she asked some of the residents of that town who had just returned from war whether her boy had died as a worthy son of Sparta. They exaggerated the facts and told her:

"He died a hero, and Sparta has not his like any more."

"Do not say such things," Argileonis rejoined. "Brasidas was good and kind, but Sparta has many who are better."
