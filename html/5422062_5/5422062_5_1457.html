Page 11. For the information of all concerned; the Greek government enacted a law taxing all Greek subjects leaving Greece for abroad, as follows:

Men would pay five pounds (Sterling); women, ten pounds; children, three. Exceptions to this tax are made for those who are ill and go abroad for treatments; for those who live abroad; for students without means, and for special delegations.

Those who avoid payment of the tax, are taxed ten times the stipulated amount, when they return.
