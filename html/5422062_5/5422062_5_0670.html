p. 1- Chicago, May 5, 1924. Resolution Adopted by the Administrative Council, on May 1, 1924.

In quorum and on motion of Mr. Giannakopoulos,the council unanimously decided to conform to the telegraphic order of the Holy Synod concerning the dependence of St. Constantine's Church and to announce this to the congregation.

The church will always comply with the orders of the Holy Synod of Greece concerning religious matters.

The telegraphic communication of the Holy Synod, which as transmitted by the Minister of Foreign Affairs, Mr. Roussos, to the consulate, reads as follows:

"No 1377. General Consulate of Greece, Chicago, April 7, 1924.

2

"Mr. Theodore, president of the Greek Community of the South Side.

"Mr. President: His Excellency Mr. Roussos, Minister of Foreign Affairs of Greece, in his telegram of April 3 of this year transmitted to us the following telegram of the Holy Metropolitan of Athens for communication to the Greek communities of Chicago which had telegraphed to the Holy Synod asking whether the Holy Synod recognizes the Archbishop of America:

"Until final arrangement of the Greek Orthodox Church in America the Holy Synod recognizes Archbishop Alexander, sent by this synod, as representing the Greek Orthodox Church in America."

Yours respectfully,

"L. Matles, Consul General."

The Administrative Council of St. Constantine's Church:
