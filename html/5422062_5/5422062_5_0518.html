p. 1.- The Trianon was transformed last Monday to a center of the Greek Community where the most select of the Greek populace was present. The masquerade costumes were not so much of a hit this year despite the prizes offered by the society, and the judges found themselves in a predicament in bestowing the awards.

The exceptional event of the evening, however, was the hymn of the organization which was sung for the first time. The music for this hymn was composed by our master musician, S. Bekatoros, and the poem by the inspired poet, lawyer, and author, Andrew Vlahos, the "Song of Songs of the Greek Race."
