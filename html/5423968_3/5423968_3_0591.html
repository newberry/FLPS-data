In the municipal elections in Chicago the Poles gained an unusual victory.

Thanks to the close cooperation of all Poles, we succeeded in electing five Polish aldermen.

We may be very proud of this; but, as the Polish papers here emphasize, are these newly elected aldermen going to be a credit to our community, or rather a disgrace?

This is a vital question. We have here Judge Jarecki, who through his honesty won a good name for himself and our community. We shall be very glad if we can say the same about the new aldermen.
