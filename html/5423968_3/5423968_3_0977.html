The Polish Democratic Club or the Seventeenth Ward held a meeting yesterday at Kalisz's Hall, corner of Front and Sangamon Streets.

L. J. Kalisz, president of the Club, opened the meeting with a short address and introduced Peter Kiolbassa as the first speaker. The latter's eloquence carried the audience, which applauded him vigorously.

Other speakers were John Gaynor, candidate for State senator; William Burke; Francis Bragnadelo; and Joseph Dombrowski. The hall was filled with Democrats.

The chairman closed the meeting by urging the Poles to vote the Democratic ticket.

Next Tuesday all Poles will undoubtedly vote for Democrats...
