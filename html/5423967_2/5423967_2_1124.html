On November 4, 1929, at the Schoenhofen Hall, a concert-ball of the Rassviet was arranged. In the program there took part the famous violinist Misha Lifshiz; the well known baritone, L. Brunwald; the virtuoso of the balalaika, V. Dobrowotov; the American singer Mary Shipler, who had a great success; the three Russian pianists Nadya Rementshik, Miss Horvich and especially the youthful Eugenie Murey, who played excellently. The diverse and interesting program was concluded with the performance of P. M. Makarova, who charmed the audience with her folk-songs. The concert was followed by dancing, which continued till late at night.
