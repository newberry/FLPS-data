The Russian colony had the pleasure of greeting a rare guest, Alexander Alexandrovich Bublikov, who was a member of the state Imperial Duma, and the first minister of communications after the overthrow of the autocracy.

A. A. Bublikov, after being commissioned by the revolutionary committee, had arrested Tsar Nikolas.

Mr. Bublikov was invited to Chicago by the Section of the Russian 'Zayem Svobody' (Liberty Loan). In this behalf, on Wednesday evening, May 1, in Pulaski Hall, there was held a grand meeting. The meeting was called to order by the chairman, Dr. Krasnow, who explained the purpose of the meeting, introducing A. A. Bublikov.

2

At the meeting, among other things, an American soldier who had just returned from the trenches delivered a speech.

The Russian choir under the direction of Mr. Rutkovsky performed nicely a few songs, among which they also sang the Russian and American national anthems.

The subscriptions have been brought near to $10,000,000.

A. A. Bublikov left for New York on the following day.
