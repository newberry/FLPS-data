My dear Grigory Romanovich:

Do not judge me severly. The cares of tomorrow, the wreched nerves and the aching soul finally got me. I cannot live any longer. I am tired. Good-bye. Forgive me.

Ivan Churinov.

(Editor's Note: Letterwritten by Russian Poet to Dr. Krasnow before his suicide in 1933.)

My Epitaph

Good-bye beautiful created world.

The Sun, bright and blue skys,

Earthly paradise and fragrance of spring

And all the beauty of mother nature,

I leave you voluntarily

For the deep darkness and endless cold.

The time is here and the verdict of my fate

That the eternal sleep should close my eyes.
