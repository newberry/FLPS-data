The author mentions the visit of Mrs. E. K. Breshko-Breshkovskaya, the "grandmother of the Russion revolution", to Chicago in 1904. She spoke at massmeetings attended by thousands of people, explaining the aims of the Social Revolutionist party in Russia.

( Note: Mrs. Breshko-Breshkovskaya visited Chicago also in 1917, after the March revolution, and spoke to large and enthusiastic audiences D.S.).
