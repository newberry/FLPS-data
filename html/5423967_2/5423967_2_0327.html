The author recalls that recently the federal court in Cleveland sentenced Debs to ten years in prison. Debs has a spirit of crystal clearness, believes what he practices and practices what he believes.

"Characteristically, Debs did not deny his 'crime,' expressing his readiness to bear the cross for his principles, his connections." He looms as a greater man than ever before. Whether one agrees with him or not, he must be respected. What a fine world this would be if all were Debses or followed Debs.
