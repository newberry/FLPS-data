Two hundred and fifty delegates attended the conference of the Defense League Against Pogroms, held last night in the West Side Auditorium. The meeting voted to call a national convention in Chicago for all anti-pogrom groups. It was voted also that the Defense League should be a permanent organization and that its membership should be open to all organizations.

At last night's meeting 41 organizations, 6 ledges, 20 societies, 3 unions, 2 socialist joined the league; altogether the league has a membership of 165 organizations.
