In the political movements for the abolition of slavery, the Jews took a leading part in creating public opinion. As early as 1853, a fugitive Negro, arrested by a U. S. Marshal, was liberated by a crowd of Chicago citizens led by Michael Greenebaum.

In the evening of that day a meeting was held to ratify that act. The first official call to organize the abolition movement was signed by George Schneider, Adolph Loeb, Julius Rosenthal, Leopold Mayer, and a man named Hanson......four Jews among the five leaders of the German population of Chicago in a great humanitarian gesture.
