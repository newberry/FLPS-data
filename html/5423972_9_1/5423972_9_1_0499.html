Dr. Louis Mann of New Haven, Connecticut, has been appointed to succeed the late Emil G. Hirsch as rabbi of the Sinai Temple. The appointment was made Sunday at the annual meeting of the Congregation. Moses E. Greenbaum was re-elected president at the same time.

Rabbi Mann, 33, graduated from the Hebrew Union College in Cincinnati and received his Ph.D. from Johns Hopkins University. His salary as rabbi of Sinai Temple will be $15,000 a year.

In honor of Dr. Emil G. Hirsch, the Sinai Social Center will henceforth be called the Emil Hirsch Social Center. Mrs. Emil G. Hirsch will receive a lifelong pension of $5,000 a year.
