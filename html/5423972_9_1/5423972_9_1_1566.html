Bernard Horwich, who, thirty years ago, founded in Chicago the first Zionist body in the U. S., was the honor guest of the Zionist Organization of Chicago, on September 8. He is a former election commissioner of Cook County, leading founder of the Federated Jewish Charities, and chairman of the People's War Relief Committee, which raised $2,000,000 in Chicago.

In 1897, together with Leon Zolotkoff, he organized the Order of Knights of Zion, the first Zionist organization in America, and became its first president. This body for many years covered the entire middle West, eventually becoming part of the Zionist Organization of America.
