The Department of Commerce announces that, according to the census of 1920, the number of people included in the foreign white stock of Chicago whose mother tongue was reported as Yiddish and Hebrew, increased from 110,089 to 159,518 or at the rate of 44.9 per cent.

Yiddish speaking Jews constituted 6.5 per cent of the Chicago population in 1910 and 8.2 per cent in 1920. The total of foreign born Jews is 87,888 while those born in America with one or both parents of foreign birth number 71,720.
