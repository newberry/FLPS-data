From the newly published Hoffman's Catholic Directory For 1896, of the Chicago Archdiocese, we have obtained the following particulars about Polish parishes and the priests assigned to them.

This list of parishes and priests in Chicago is as follows:

St. Adalbert Parish: Reverend John Radziejewski and Reverend Truszynski.

St. Peter And Paul Parish: Reverend Paul Rhode.

St. Casimir Parish: Reverend Albert Furman.

St. Hedwig Parish: Reverend John Piechowski and Reverend Biela.

Holy Trinity Parish: Reverend Casimir Sztuczko and Reverend E. Raczynski.

St. Hyacinth Parish (Avondale): Reverend Joseph Gieburowski.

St. John Cantius Parish: Reverend John Kasprzycki, Reverend Fluryan Matuszewjki and Reverend Anzelm Babski.

St. Josephat Parish: Reverend Francis Lange and Reverend 'B. Nowakowski.

2

Holy Mary Of Czestochowa Parish: Reverend Nawrocki and Reverend Byrgier.

St. Michael Archangel Parish (South Chicago): Reverend Adolph Nowicki and Reverend Gurowski.

St. Stanislaus Kostka Parish: Reverend Simon Kobrzynski, Reverend Vincent Barzynski, Reverend John Barzynski, Reverend Eugene Sedlaczek, Reverend L. Machdzicki, Reverend Vincent Rapacz, Reverend John Kruszynski, Reverend J. Obyrtowicz, and Reverend F. Breitkopf.

St. Joseph Parish: Reverend Michel Pyplatz.

Immaculate Conception of the Blessed Virgin Mary Parish: Reverend Francis Wojtalewicz.

St. Stanislaus Bishop and Martyr Parish (Cragin): Reverend Theophil Szypkowski.

Altogether we have in Chicago (including Cicero and South Chicago) fifteen Polish parishes and thirty-one priests.

In parishes outside of Chicago we have the following priests:

Sobieski: Reverend Zaleski.

Lemont: Reverend C. Kozlowski.

Downer's Grove (Gostyn): Reverend Chodziewicz.

Joliet: Reverend B. Pawlowski.

The number of children attending the Polish Parochial Schools according to the directory is as follows:

At St. Adalbert: 975; at St. Peter and Paul: 50; at St. Casimir: 208; at St. Hedwig: 761; at Holy Trinity: 437; at St. Hyacinth: 80; at St. John Cantius: 628; at St. Josephat: 524; at St. Joseph: 483; in Cicero: 70; at the Holy Mary of Perpetual Help school: 805; at St. Michael in South Chicago: 740; at St. Stanislaus Kostka: 3,550; at the Immaculate Conception of the Blessed Virgin Mary (South Chicago): 600; in Sobieski: 50; in Lemont: 282; in Joliet: 92.

We are publishing these figures just as they are printed in the directory, 4but, at the same time, we wish to state that they seem to us quite low. In all probability, the number of Polish school children attending the Polish parochial schools in Chicago is considerably greater. We ask the reverend pastors to notify us of any mistakes in the list; we will gladly publish the correct figures.
