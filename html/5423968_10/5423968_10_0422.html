Reverend Eugene Sedlaczek, who recently returned from a stay in Europe, has been appointed pastor of the St. Hyacinth Church by the archbishop of Chicago. The new pastor celebrated his initial Mass yesterday [Sunday] at the church, and the former pastor, Reverend Joseph Gieburowski, delivered a farewell address to his parishioners. His words touched the hearts of the entire congregation.

After the services a general meeting of the parishioners was held to pay tribute to their old pastor, whom they held until he promised that he would pay them a visit next month.

The change was made at the parish because of the ill health of the Reverend Gieburowski. Rather than have the parish without a pastor during the long absence of the stricken priest, a successor was appointed. In order to quiet any disturbance that may have been aroused, a committee was chosen to make the decision and introduce the new pastor.

No doubt is held but that the Reverend Sedlaczek will continue the good work that he has been doing at St. Stanislaus Kostka parish. May God preserve the new pastor.
