The immigration statute requiring the reading test for all immigrants was finally defeated in Congress and will not be raised any more at this session. The lower House of Congress upheld the veto of President Wilson by a small majority.

On Feb. 4, at 5 o'clock, a vote was cast on this statute. In the lower House of Congress an effort was made to vote down the Presidential veto and out of 399 members of this House, 261 voted against the Presidential veto, and 136 supported the President. The opposers of free immigration lacked 5 votes of the required 73 majority.
