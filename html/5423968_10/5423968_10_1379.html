Below we place two telegrams: one from the administration of the Polish Roman-Catholic Union to President Wilson, on the occasion of its installation, expressing loyalty and gratitude for the stand he has taken towards Poland, the second from President Wilson to the administration of the Polish Roman-Catholic Union, in which, through his secretary, he sends thanks for the hearty expressions of readiness and gratitude. Below are the contents:

"Honorable Woodrow Wilson

President of the United States

Washington, D. C.

"Dear Mr. President: We, the officers and members of the Executive Board of the Polish Roman-Catholic Union of North America, having this day been inaugurated and having assumed our respective offices, extend to you, 2Mr. President, and to your administration, our loyalty and hearty support and the loyalty of the 100,000 members belonging to our organization.

"In this connection permit us also to express to you our deepest gratitude for espousing the cause of unfortunate Poland. Please accept, Mr. President, sentiments of profound respect, loyalty and devotion."

N. L. Piotrowski

President

I. Wroblewski

General Secretary.

3

"The White House

Washington, Feb. 9, 1918.

"My dear Mr. Piotrowski: The President very deeply appreciates the heartening assurance which you and Mr. Wroblewski give him in the name of the Polish Catholic Union of America, and he asks me to convey to you and to everyone concerned an expression of his cordial thanks. He is gratful for your support.

Sincerely yours,

W. Tumulty

Secretary to the President.

"Mr. N. L. Piotrowski

Chicago, Illinois."
