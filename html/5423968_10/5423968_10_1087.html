There are very few Polish male religious institutions in America. Of these, there is only one in Chicago--that of the Resurrection Fathers, whose superior is Father Simon Kobrzynski.

Female Polish religious establishments are far more numerous. They exist beside practically every Polish parochial school, and a detailed list of them will be found below. We need but note here that the education of Polish children in Chicago is principally in the hands of the Felician, Franciscan, Nazarene, and Notre Dame Sisters.

2

As to Polish Catholic educational and charitable institutions, they are as follows:

The Holy Family Polish-Bohemian Orphanage, under the guidance of the Notre Dame Sisters, and the high school for Polish girls, under the guidance of the Nazarene Sisters.

The [parochial] school of St. Mary of Perpetual Help (Felician Sisters and lay teachers), 218 boys and 203 girls, or 423 [sic] pupils.

In South Chicago the [parochial] School of St. Mary of Immaculate Conception (Sisters and lay teachers) is attended by 250 boys and 300 girls, or 550 pupils.

The total number of children in the Polish parochial schools in Chicago and South Chicago reaches at least 6,069.
