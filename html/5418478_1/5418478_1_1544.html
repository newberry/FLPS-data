In the past meeting of the Cicero Board of Trustees, Mr. Joseph Z. Kleňha presiding . . . . .

As it is known, the Czechoslovak Taxpayers' Association of Cicero protested against the bad conditions of the street car tracks on Twenty-fifth Street in Cicero, causing many accidents. The protest was sent by Mr. Frank Houček, town clerk, to the Chicago West Town Railway Company. The company now replied that some improvement was made, but because of unfavorable weather conditions, the company was not able to continue . . . . .
