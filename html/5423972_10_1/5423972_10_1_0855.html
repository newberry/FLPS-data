Rabbi Joseph N. Newberger, founder of two Chicago synagogues, has retired at the age of 72 and departed for Palestine, where he will spend the remainder of his life.

Rabbi Newberger came to Chicago in 1892. He became a match peddler to support himself as he founded Synagogue B'nai Itzchok at Morgan and Maxwell streets. In 1912 he organized a second synagogue, Attereth Israel, at 1230 S. Millard Ave. He has also been a director of Beth Midrash Le Torah, the only post-graduate rabbinical college in the city.
