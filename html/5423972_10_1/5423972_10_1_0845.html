Leopold Hubschman, died last Friday. He was born in Hungary, April 12, 1860. He came to the United States at the age of twenty-five and three years later, (1888), he settled in Chicago. Here he engaged in the shoe business and then in the manufacture of clothing following which, he became a gem merchant, in which occupation he continued until his death.

His chief communal interest was the Agudath Achim Congregation, which he served as president for a number of years and was instrumental in the building of the synagogue on Kenmore Avenue.
