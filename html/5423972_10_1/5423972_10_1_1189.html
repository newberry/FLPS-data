Joseph Block, one of America's pioneers in the steel business, is dead. He was born on May 13, 1831, at Phaffenhofen, France and came to the United States with his parents at the age of 7.

Mr. Block's business career began in Cincinnati with the founding of Block and Pollock in the early '60s. This company became the Cincinnati Forge and Iron Works and eventually was incorporated into the Block-Pollock Iron Company of Chicago. In 1893, Mr. Block helped organize the Inland Steel Company at Indiana Harbor and became a director. He was also a director of the Buffalo Steel Company at Tonawanda, N. Y.

He was a member of the Standard and Lake Shore Country clubs.
