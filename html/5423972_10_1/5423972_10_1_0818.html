Leo Kantrow, for a number of years a familiar figure in West Side banking circles, died last week.

Mr. Kantrow was born in Nemiroff, Russia, April 18, 1896. He obtained his education first in Kiev and then in Odessa. In his 22nd year, he came to the United States and took up residence in Chicago. Mr. Kantrow was vice president of the State Savings Bank & Trust Company and has been identified with that bank before it reorganized for the last 14 years.

He was a member of the Ramah Lodge of B'nai B'rith, Majestic Lodge of the Masonic Order, and treasurer of the Chicago Consumptive Aid Society.
