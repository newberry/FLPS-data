Mrs. Nelson Morris, widow of the packer, died in France as a result of an automobile accident on Sept. 16. After her husband's death two years ago, when she became the possessor of $6,000,000 of his estate, Mrs. Morris became known as a generous giver to charitable institutions. Within the last three weeks, announcement had been made of her gift of $250,000 to establish an institute of scientific research.

Mrs. Morris was the daughter of Abram Vogel, one of the early shippers of live stock from the Chicago market. She was born in Chicago in 1845. Her love for children led her to make large contributions to charitable enterprises directly affecting children.
