Judge Martin J. Isaacs, Superior Court, died, Monday evening, from a heart attack. He was fifty-nine years old.

Judge Isaacs was born in Memphis, Tenn., and was admitted to the bar in 1891. He engaged in practice at Plano for two years, then moved to Chicago, where he resided until his death.

He was a member of the law firm of Silber, Isaacs, Silber & Woley, and a member of the Chicago Bar Association, and served for 17 years as a Master-in-Chancery before his election to the Superior Court bench in November, 1928.
