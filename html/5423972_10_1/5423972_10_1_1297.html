Lazarus Silverman, age 79, died Wednesday afternoon. The deceased occupied a prominent position in the growth of Chicago and in the banking history of the country. His counsel was sought and his friendship was prized by such men as Philip D. Armour, Potter Palmer, Marshall Field, Otto Young and George M. Pullman. For more than 56 years Mr. Silverman lived in Chicago. The deceased was born in Oberschwavzaz, Bavaria, in 1830, and came to America at the age of 19.
