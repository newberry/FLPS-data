Mr. Isaac Greensfelder was born in Lehrberg, Bavaria, in 1827. In 1853 he came to Chicago. In October 1859, he was one of the active leaders in the Hebrew Relief Association. For thirty-three years he has been an officer of this society, and its president for thirty-one years, a position which he still holds.

He is a charter member of Sinai Congregation, and for many years, one of its directors. As president of the United Hebrew Charities, he also has the Michael Reese Hospital under his official management. He is a director of the Jewish Orphan Home, and a member of the Standard Club.
