Abraham Hart died yesterday, one of the founders of the United Jewish Welfare Associations. In former years, he participated at all welfare endeavors. He was the first president of the Board of Directors of the Michael Reese Hospital. To the Jewish Orphan Home in Cleveland, he was especially bound, from 1870 till 1896 he belonged to its committees and until his death he was one of its directors.

Mr. Hart, born in Germany in 1831, came to Chicago in 1854 and by his great business ability, became the owner of a large Wholesale Tailoring House. He leaves a daughter, Mrs. Jacob Levi and two sons, Harry and Milton.
