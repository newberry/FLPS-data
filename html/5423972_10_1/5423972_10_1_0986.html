Jacob Ringer, one of the best known lawyers in Chicago, is dead. He was prominent in cases before the Circuit Court for the last few years. His firm was employed by the city in the telephone rate cases. Prior to Oct. 1919, when he began work for the city, Mr. Ringer took no part in politics. In 1921 he was on the Thompson-Lundin Circuit court judicial ticket.

He was formerly president of the Standard Club.
