Owing to the fact, that new arrivals of Russian refugees are but few, and that for the relief of the resident poor, there is no necessity for two societies with two separate and expensive offices, and employees. The executive board of this society, after nature deliberation, have resolved to turn over our affairs to the United Hebrew Charities, subject to a vote of ratification by those who have contributed towards this charity.

Adolph Loeb, president,

Milton J. Foreman, secretary.
