An issue of the Jewish Review, Cleveland, contained the following item of interest in regard to Mr. A. E. Frankland of Chicago. -

"A great many people remember him as the 'Hero of Memphis' during the terrible epidemic of yellow fever in that city. While thousands of inhabitants, and even the city officers fled from the stricken city, he remained, practically taking control of the city affairs and rendered such noble assistance that the papers of the country wrote of him as the 'Hero of Memphis.' "
