Samuel Deutsch died on Wednesday at his home. He was born in Louisville, Kentucky, June 1, 1865, and came to Chicago in 1899.

Upon his arrival in Chicago, he became interested in the Chicago Home For Jewish Orphans, of which he was made a director. He also served a term as president of the Associated Jewish Charities, and has been president of the Standard Club, and vice president of the Federated Charities.

Beginning with 1917 and until the close of the war - relief work in 1922, Mr. Deutsch was one of the main stays of the Chicago Jewish Relief Committee.

Mr. Deutsch was a member of the Executive Committee of the Inland Steel Company and affiliated with other large industrial enterprises.
