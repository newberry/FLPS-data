Isaac Woolf, president of Woolf's Clothing Co., died Sunday morning, October 21, at his residence, 3431 Michigan Ave. The deceased was born in London, on January 3, 1853, and came to this country when a child with his parents. He settled in Chicago, where he was employed as a retail salesman by the Barbe Bros. Clothing-House. In 1880 he went into business with his brothers at 183 West Madison Street.

Although the Annual Dinner for Newsboys was Mr. Woolf's favorite philanthropic enterprise, he also gave generously to Hebrew Charities. He was a member of Sinai Congregation, and belonged to the Standard Club, Masons, and the Royal Arcanum.
