Charles Kozminski was born June 12, 1836, in the Prussian province of Silesia. He came to Chicago in 1854. In the fifties he was the first president of the first German Republican organization in Chicago, known as the Washington Club.

In 1887 he was appointed by Mayor Roche, a member of the Board of Education and, as Chairman of the Finance Committee, he proved himself an active and useful member. He was one of the main factors in securing the passage of the compulsory education law. He was connected with the United Hebrew Relief Association and held different offices in the organization. He was trustee from 1869 to 1871; financial secretary from 1871 to 1873, and president from 1873 to 1874.
