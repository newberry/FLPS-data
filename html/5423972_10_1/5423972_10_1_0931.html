Albert Henry Loeb, died on October 27. He was born February 18, 1868 at Rockford, Illinois. He was a teacher in the night schools of Chicago; he studied law and was admitted to the bar in 1889. Mr. Loeb established the law firm of Loeb and Adler.

In 1901 he joined the firm of Sears, Roebuck and Company as its Secretary. In 1908 he became vice-president of the mail order house.

He was a Republican and a thirty-second degree Mason. He was also a member of the Standard Ravisloe and Lake Shore Country Clubs.
