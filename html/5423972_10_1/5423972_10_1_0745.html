Isadore Cohen, for many years an outstanding leader in the religious, cultural and philanthropic life of the Chicago Jewish Community, died Wednesday morning.

He was born in Elizabethgrad, Russia, in 1874, and at the age of 17, came to Chicago. He was a commission merchant in food supplies. He was a frequent contributor to the market pages of the Chicago Daily newspapers, trade topics, upon which he was regarded as an authority.

Mr. Cohen was a Director of the Marks Nathan Orphan Home, the Mt. Sinai Hospital, the Hebrew Theological College. He was also a member of the Chicago Association of Commerce, and the Chicago Art Institute.
