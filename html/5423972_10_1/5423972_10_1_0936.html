Dr. Jacob W. Bolotin, well-known in medical and welfare circles, died on April 1, at the age of thirty-six. He received his education at the State School for the Blind at Jacksonville, Illinois, and graduated from the Chicago College of Medicine and Surgery in1912.

For two years Dr. Bolotin was an instructor in surgical diagnosis. He was tuberculosis consultant for the United States Veteran Bureau Hospital at Speedway, and counsel for the Livingston County Tuberculosis Sanitarium at Pontiac, and the Highland La Salle County Tuberculosis Sanitarium at Ottawa, Illinois.

He was a member of the American Medical Association, Chicago Medical Association, State Medical Society, Chicago Tuberculosis Society, and Mutual Benefit Association for the Blind.

Dr. Bolotin was the organizer of a troop of blind boy scouts. He was past Lieutenant Governor of Illinois and the Eastern Ohio District of the Kiwanis Club, and trustee of the West Side Kiwanis Club.
