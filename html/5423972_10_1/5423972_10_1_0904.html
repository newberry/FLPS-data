Mr. Judah Loeb Yudelson, one of the oldest Jews in Chicago, was buried on July 22. He was one of the remaining few of the type of Jew which is rapidly passing from our midst. His life characterized profound love of Jewish learning and modesty. He was born in Suvaiki, June 22, 1835.

He came to the United States in 1890, and settled in Chicago shortly after his arrival to these shores.
