William K. Ackerman, an old, well-known Swedish-American of the Chicago colony died last Tuesday at his home, 404 N. State Street.... The deceased was city comptroller under Mayor Hopkins' administration, but he gained greater renown as auditor for the World's Fair in Chicago, in 1893. His appointment to this position occurred in 1890, and he continued in this position until the books of the Fair had been closed and audited, doing his work to the full satisfaction of those concerned.
