R.H. Johnson, who for twenty-five years has been employed at the Northwestern Railroad, and during the last five years as superintendent for the freight stations was honored last Saturday with a banquet at the Sherman House given by forty railroad foremen, agents, and others, who had been his subordinates. The purpose of the feast was to bid him goodbye as he is leaving his position, and will move to Peoria to be president for the Peoria-Pekin Union railroad. He received a diamond pin, valued at $300 a token of respect, at the banquet. Mrs. Johnson received a set of silver.
