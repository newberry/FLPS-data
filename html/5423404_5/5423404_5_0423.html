Emanuel Nylin, vice-president of the Peoples National Bank and Trust Company here, has been made a member of the Board of Directors of the newly formed Standard National Bank of Chicago, which opens for business next Saturday, September 14, in temporary quarters at Ashland Avenue and 79th Street.

The new bank is capitalized at $300,000, with reserves of $50,000, and has already purchased a building lot next to its present location, on which a modern bank building is to be erected.

Nylin was born in Stockholm, Sweden, and was only three years old when he came to America with his parents. For many years he has taken an active part in Swedish church work, as well as in the activities of several Swedish societies. He is an attorney and belongs to the Chicago, the Illinois, and the American Bar Associations. He is also a member of the Hamilton Club.
