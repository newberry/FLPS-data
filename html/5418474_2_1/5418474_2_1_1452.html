Louis and Meyer Marks sold the two-story building at 1642 Belmont Avenue, including the ground, 25 by 125 feet, to Ralph Kelley, dealer in paints, for an alleged price of thirty-five thousand dollars.

Paul Ohmig and Company bought the modern, fireproof factory on the northeast corner of California Avenue and Flournoy Street from the American Oven and Machine Company, for ninety thousand dollars, it is said.
