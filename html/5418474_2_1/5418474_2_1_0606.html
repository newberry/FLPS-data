The citizens League started suit against the saloons in the limits of Hyde Park which are licensed by the county commission.

The Hyde Park villiage board resolved to close all inns and saloons on Sundays after August 1st.

This resolution is an infamous dishonesty against the saloonkeepers who shortly before had to pay an excessive extra tax to that village treasury for dispensary licenses.

Everybody knows that these inns in outlying communities do their business only on Sundays or holidays and can pay their taxes only through that particular Sunday business.

It would have been better to declare Hyde Park a part of the city of Chicago.

In that case Chicagoans would not be subjected to the whims of a village burgomaster when visiting the parks for which they pay taxes.
