The society for the Danish Old People's Home has just published its 10th annual report, in which its president, Mrs. Wm. Nelson, announces that plans for a new and larger home are being made. A committee has been appointed for the purpose of raising the necessary funds, and the prospects of success are excellent.

An expansion is highly desirable, since there are a great number of worthy applicants that cannot be accommodated in the home under present conditions.

However, when building a new and larger home, the society also has to consider ways and means of maintaining it after it is built. To that end, it has been conducting a drive for more members, and last year saw an increase of 52, bringing the total, as of Jan. 1, 1913, to 310.

Its goal is to have 1,200 members on the books three years hence, when the 25th anniversary of its founding will be observed.

At the present time the society's net assets amount to $36,768.
