The Danish Worker's Singing Society competed in a singing contest last Monday in North Chicago, in which seventeen singing societies, representing as many nationalities, participated.

Among the larger societies the German Wagner took first prize and the Norwegian Bjorgvin took second prize, while among the smaller ones the Danish singers were awarded first prize, a beautiful cup.

The Danes became even more popular when it was discovered that Mayor M. Christensen, of North Chicago, is a Dane. He delivered a speech in which he complimented his countrymen on their singing.

North Chicago is supposedly "dry" but arrangements had been made so that the singers did not have to go thirsty, and they spent a very pleasant day in Mayor Christensen's town.
