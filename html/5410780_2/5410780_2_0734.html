The magnetic attraction was so strong that the house was filled to capacity for the singing society Magneten's presentation of Blinkenberg-Jensen's latest dramatic effort, "A Wonderful Invention."

If you did not go expecting to be deeply moved by a sensational drama, but rather something light and extremely funny, you could not possibly be disappointed. The plot was really original and was ably executed by the society's clever dramatic talent. We particularly wish to compliment Mrs. Blinkenberg-Jensen for her work as "Mrs. Hansen, and Emanuel Olson for his splendid characterization of the harmonica-playing janitor.

After the performance everybody enjoyed a couple of hours dancing and the famous sandwiches in the basement.
