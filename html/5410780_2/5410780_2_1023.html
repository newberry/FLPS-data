"Jylland Mellem Tvende Have" has been translated by Alfred G. Andersen and given the title "Song of Jutland." The translation is good, and is as follows:

Jutland, ocean-skirted haven,

Is a runestave 'tween the seas,

Dolmens are the runes engraven

Deep among the forests trees

And upon the ample floor

Of the mirage-haunted mow.

Jutland! Thou art Denmark's mother:

Heights where forest stillness bides;

Westward, piled on one another,

Rugged down the mountain sides.

Baltic wave and North Sea meet

O'er the sand at Skaen's feet.

2

On the moor, nigh past believing,

See yourself this virgin ground,

Heather-decked and softly heaving,

Blossom strewed for miles around.

Hurry hither! Come before

Grainfields drape the dwindling moor.

Jutland, ocean-skirted haven,

Is a runestave 'tween the seas:

Cairns with love of foretime laden,

Roots of future growth and peace.

East and west the breakers roar

A tune to the Jutland shore.

Hans Christian Anderson.
