A group of Danish bankers and business men are paying America a visit. The Danish colony will arrange a banquet in their honor at the Bismarck Hotel. Forty-three courses are to be served. The committee for this affair consists of the following: Dr. Max Henius, president; Ditlew M. Frederiksen, secretary and Oscar Aalberg, treasurer. There will be two special invited guests: Counsul Soegaard, Louisville, Kentucky and Consul P. B. Nelson, Racine, Wisconsin. Consul C. H. Hansen will welcome the guests. The banquet is limited to 70.
