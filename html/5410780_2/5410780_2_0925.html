Magneten gave a very enjoyable and successful evening of entertainment last Sunday in Bjorgvin's Hall, with the presentation of the two plays, "The Two Deaf-Mutes" and "Peter's Masterpiece."

The singing society's best dramatic talent was in the firing line, and in spite of many imperfections, the actors were generously applauded by the capacity house; in fact so enthusiastic was the response that Magneten's dramatic department was encouraged to replot the performance in the near future, probably in the new Dania Hall.

Hans Schmidt's dance orchestra was excellent as usual, and Magneten's ladies had arranged a very tasty buffet supper.

The ivory president's club donated by Viego Jensen was won by lodge No. 18, 2and netted a nice little sum for the building fund.
