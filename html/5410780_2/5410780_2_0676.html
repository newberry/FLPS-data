On April 24, 1932, in Harmonien's Hall, Harmonien held a contest between the different choruses that had represented the Society. The choruses which took part in the contest were the Schoenhofen Chorus, made up of all members who had sung in the [Harmonien] chorus for at least twenty-five years; the Muskegon Chorus, which was sent to that city in 1921; the San Francisco Chorus, which was sent to the World's Fair of 1915; the Denmark Chorus of 1924; the Denmark Chorus of 1930; and the Harmonien Chorus of 1932.

Joel Mossberg [Swede] was director.
