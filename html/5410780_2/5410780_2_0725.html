The Harmonien Singing Society sent its San Francisco Chorus to the San Francisco World's Fair in 1915. For three years they had prepared for this event. Dr. [Max] Henius and Mr. Josef Jensen helped them with their expenses. The Chorus left Chicago May 15. On its way, the Chorus sang in Omaha, Nebraska, and Salt Lake City, Utah.

On June 5 they took part in the Danish National Holiday at the World's Fair. On June 6 they sang with two other singing societies, Brage, of Oakland, and Lyren, of San Francisco. On this occasion it was the Chicago Chorus that received the most applause.

The Chorus left San Francisco on June 10. The members will long cherish the memories of this trip.
