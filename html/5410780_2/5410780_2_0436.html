Mr. M. Olson, was sent to Kankakee, yesterday. Mr. Olson, has been with the South Chicago Street Railway Company, for more than twelve years. He was the inventor of the cablecar, used here in Chicago. He also invented the boiler and engine in use by the various street railways here.

In 1893, he obtained a patent, on a railway truck, that is in use throughout the states.

Mr. Olsen's illness was caused by over-work.
