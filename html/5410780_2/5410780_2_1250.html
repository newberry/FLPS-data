The Danish Rifle Club observed the 23rd anniversary of its founding last Saturday with a banquet in Metropolitan Hall.

The attendance was unexpectedly large, and last-minute arrangements had to be made in order to accommodate the many members and friends who wished to help the club pass this milestone.

Among the speakers were the champion sharpshooter Westergaard and the track star Christensen of the Danish-American Athletic Club, who suggested a merger of the two organizations. The club's honorary member, the battle-scarred professional boxer "Battling" Nelson, was accorded an ovation fit for a king when he addressed the gathering and recounted a few humorous episodes from his fighting career.

Good sportsmanship and joviality characterized the celebration, which continued into the wee hours.
