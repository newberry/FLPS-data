Eight of the active singers of the Magneten Singing Society, the chorus of the Danish Brotherhood in Chicago, joined the colors during the World War. The chorus was so crippled that it found it impossible to carry on. In February, 1920, it merged with the Dansk Arbejder Sangforening (the Danish Workingmen's Singing Society).

At that time the three Danish singing societies in Chicago, Harmonien, Magneten, and Dansk Arbejder Sangforening, had been trying to form a united chorus. Magneton's collapse and merger with Dansk Arbejder Sangforening, and the fact that Harmonien was making preparations for its trip to Denmark, caused a great deal of misunderstanding.
