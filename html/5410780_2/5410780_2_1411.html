Denmark Lodge No. 908 of the Odd Fellow Order observed the 10th anniversary of its founding last Saturday with a festive gathering in Walhalla Hall. The large attendance testified to the steady progress this lodge has made, particularly during the last few years.

Noble Grand Christensen delivered the welcoming speech and appointed Carl W. Larson toastmaster; he had quite a job keeping track of all the speakers.

The Danish author and poet, L. C. Nielsen, who is in America on a lecture tour, was present as a special guest, and was heartily feted by his Chicago countrymen.

Between speeches, M. Henningsen entertained the guests by showing and 2explaining some of Dr. Henius' famous lantern slides; a team of folk dancers demonstrated a number of old Danish dances, and finally a few lodge members, dressed in the uniform of the old Guard Corps, with bearskin caps and all other accessories, marched around the dining room, led by a drummer, beating his drum. They were wildly applauded.

A fine printed program had been prepared for the occasion, containing a brief summary of the history of the lodge and pictures of its founders.
