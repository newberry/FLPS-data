The Chicago Daily News insists that the American congress between 1868 and 1872 made a great mistake by not ratifying the treaty between Denmark and the United States as to the sale of the two Danish West Indian islands: St. Thomas and St. Jan, for the sum of $7,500,000. These islands would have been of great importance to the United States as coaling stations during the war with Spain.

Furthermore that the American nation had greatly insulted the Danish government by not accepting the offer.
