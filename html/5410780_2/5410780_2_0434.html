The old Danish hotel Dannevirke on Milwaukee Avenue will soon go out of existence. The hotel was bought in 1873 or 1874 by Mr. C. Hansen, who previously had a hotel with the same name on West Kinzie Street. During the World's Columbian Exposition he was offered $10,000 for the establishment, and shortly after Mr. Hansen's death his wife was offered $2,500.

The neighborhood has changed. At one time there were many Scandinavians there, but more and more Jews and Southern Europeans have moved in.

Mrs. Hansen has sold all the furniture, and has moved to Rockwell Street, where she has opened a boardinghouse for her old friends.
