Mr. Th. Otterstrom, who last year justified his fame as a ranking composer at a concert given by the Chicago Symphony Orchestra, won new laurels last Sunday when his latest composition, a violin sonata, was presented before a large and distinguished audience at the Fine Arts Theatre.

The composition was played with perfect techinque by the well-known and popular violinist Ludwig Becker, with piano accompaniment by the composer.

Of the sonata's four parts, the third, built around an Indian motif, merits special mention, on account of its strange beauty.

The composition was received with unmixed enthusiasm, and Mr. Otterstrom took the bow several times.
