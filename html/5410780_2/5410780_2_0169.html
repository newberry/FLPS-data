In his last message to Congress, President Wilson characterized the monopolies as an "insufferable nuisance." If he really means that, and we think he does, and acts accordingly, he should be able to administer the death blow to the so-called "free competition," but he had better be prepared for stubborn opposition from the reactionary elements within his own party who do not with any essential changes in the present system.

However, Mr. Wilson is not a political juggler of Roosevelt's type, but possesses both the will and the ability to introduce, at least partly, such reforms as he believes are needed.
