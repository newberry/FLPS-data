On November 8, 1936, Harmonien celebrated its fiftieth anniversary with a concert and banquet at the Lions Club, at 4306 West North Avenue.

Seven hundred people attended the concert presented by Hans Schmidt's Orchestra, under the direction of Joel Mossberg [Swede], at 3:30 P.M. P. Marinus Paulsen directed while his own compositions were being played.

The banquet, which was held at 7 P.M., was attended by six hundred guests. L. Hannemann spoke on behalf of Harmonien, Jacob Enemark, of Denmark, George P. Jensen, of America, and Balk Miller, on behalf of the ladies.

Thirty-eight telegrams were received.

Harmonien's Ladies' Society presented the singers with a silver lyre on which fifty silver coins were fastened. Twelve singers received the gift for having sung in the chorus twenty-five years or more.
