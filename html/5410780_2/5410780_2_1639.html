The new Danish Old People's Home was dedicated September 19, 1915. The building is located in Norwood Park, Illinois. It is a red tile and stone structure and has cost $23,000. John C. Christensen was the architect and supervised the whole work without any fee. The mason and carpentry work was done by two Swedes. Jens Lundsberg did all the cement work; J. C. Weckerley did the painting; L. A. Clausen the plumbing; C. E. Jensen all the tin work, and J. P. Rasmussen installed the heating system.

Coffee Dealer Bertel Nielsen donated linoleum and carpets for the stairs and the halls.
