Den Danske Sangforening Harmonien (the Harmonien Danish Singing Society), of Chicago, was founded in November, 1886, in Dania Hall, at the corner of Milwaukee Avenue and Kinzie Street. [Translator's note: The exact date of this meeting has never been established. In the Scrapbook November 3, November 8, and November 10 are all given as the date of the organization's founding; November 10 is the one most generally accepted.]

The board had the following members: Henry William Rocatis, president; Otlo Damm, vice-president; Marinus Olsen, secretary, and P. W. Schouboe, treasurer.
