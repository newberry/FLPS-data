page 7 column 8...P. Marinus Paulsen is one of the best known of American conductors and has, in addition, won distinction as a violinist and composer. He was born in Aalborg, Donmark, and at and early age came to Chicago, where he received his education supplemented by a short finishing course in Europe. His teachers were Race, Listenann, Palk, and Svendsen of Copenhagen. Among his larger compositions is the sacred cantata "Love Triumpant." In 1923 he won the $1,000 prize offered by the Chicago ever for the best one poem with "Four Oriental Sketches," conducting, its first performance in said theater.

Mr. Paulsen's identity with the Sherwood Music School has aided a great deal in winning success for that institution.
