Sunday afternoon, Jan. 7, the Danish Workers' Singing Society held a shooting match at Jacobsen's Hall. A large number of members and friends participated, and bombarded the birds with unfailing accuracy. The only mishap that occurred was a couple of wild bullets, going through a glass door.

The manager of Jacobsen's Hall, the strong blacksmith Chris Nielsen, whose hand is still steady after years of tending bar, was the day's best marksman and his victory won him a fine smoking set.

Supper was served from baskets brought along by the ladies, and as a finishing touch, the crowd danced to Scheel's music.
