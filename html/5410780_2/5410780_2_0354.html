Thomas Corneliuson, dairy manufacturing specialist of the Dairy Division, United States Department of Agriculture, died October 3, after a six month's illness.

Mr. Corneliuson was born in Denmark in 1865, emigrated to America in 1890, where he finished his studies at the University of Wisconsin.

In 1904-5, he became Cream Inspector for the Wisconsin State Dairymen's Association and later, State Creamery Dairy and Food Inspector of Wisconsin until 1908, when he became Dairy Specialist for the United States Department of Agriculture. Here he worked with the Dane, Helmer Rebild, chief of the Dairy extension.

Mr. Corneliuson was considered one of the greatest authorities on Butter in the United States.
