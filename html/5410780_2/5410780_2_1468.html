The yearbook published by the central committee of the Danish Brotherhood lodges in Chicago has just appeared. Copies have been sent to the 1,550 local members of the society.

The yearbook is 102 pages long. It gives brief accounts of the different Chicago lodges, a list of their members, a history of the singing society Magneten, and many advertisements.

The yearbook also contains a short statement about the employment agency maintained by the Brotherhood. From November, 1908, to May, 1909, forty people applied for work; the agency was able to place twenty-six. From May to November, 1909, forty-five applied for work, and twenty-five secured jobs. The agency is run by the secretary, Mr. C. Scheef, 1500 East 63rd Place.
