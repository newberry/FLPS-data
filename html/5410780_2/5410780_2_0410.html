A new Scandinavian bank has been opened on the corner of La Salle and Randolph Streets. It is called the Union Bank of Chicago. It has a capital of $200,000 and a surplus of $25,000. Its president is the Norwegian banker, Chas, E. Schlytern. Among the board of directors is the Dane, Fritz Frantzen. Among the stockholders are such well-known Danes and Norwegians as J. C. Eskildsen, T. C. Hansen, L. L. Filstrup, H. Finstad, Henry L. Hertz, Olaf E. Ray, and W. A. Wieboldt.
