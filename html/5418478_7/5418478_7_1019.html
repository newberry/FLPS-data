Delegates from sixty-five Protestant churches using the Czechoslovak language met in Chicago, June 25th and 26th. Presbyterians, Baptists, Methodists, Congregational and Reformed churches were represented. An organization was formed under the name, Czechoslovak Evangelical Union of America, which will devote its energies at the present time, especially, to the support of war orphans. It will also favor all efforts for deeper religious life among the liberated people. It was announced that the Presbyterians were sending Joseph Krenek of Silver Lake, Minn., as fraternal delegate to the Evangelical Church of the Brethren, and that the Baptists were sending as their delegate, Vaclav Kralicek of Chicago. Vaclav Vanek of Chicago was elected first president of the Evangelical Union.
