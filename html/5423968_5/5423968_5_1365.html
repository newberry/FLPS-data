The Polish Central Committee organized for the purpose of increasing the attendance of Poles in the Lwow Exposition held a meeting Thursday evening, December 7, at the rectory of the St. Stanislaus Kostka Parish. Mr. E. Jerzmanowski of New York, the honorary president of the committee, presided at the meeting. Mr. L. Szopinski, the secretary, submitted a general report on the progress of the committee, and then current matters were discussed. A final decision was made as to the plans for the pavilion already sent to Lwow, and it was decided to notify Professor Dunikowski of this decision by telegraph. After the meeting proper, the assembly remained and discussed many matters pertaining to the welfare of the Poles in America.
