The Polish Philharmonic Society sponsors the presentation of symphonic compositions by Polish composers to the American musical public.

The field of Polish symphonic literature is unusually rich and interesting, and thus we find that the concerts given by the Co-operative Symphony Orchestra, under the direction of Mr. Edmund Zygman, are engaging the attentions of a circle of music lovers.

The first two concerts given by the orchestra in November 1925 and May 1926 were an artistic success, and elicited much favorable criticism from professional sources.

The Polish Philharmonic Society will give several concerts during the season of 1926-1927, which will illustrate the modern trend of Polish composers like Rozychi, Karlowicz, Szymanowski, Paderewski, Fitelberg and others.
