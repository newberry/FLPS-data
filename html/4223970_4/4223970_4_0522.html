Four Lithuanian societies in Englewood created a Branch to Aid Lithuania some time ago. How many hundred dollars they have raised to help Lithuania, the Lietuva has reported previously. This small Lithuanian colony has taken an important place in Lithuania's service and, undoubtedly, when Lithuania takes stock of her benefactors in the future, much gratitude will be due to this colony because of its great effort to aid Lithuania during her greatest misfortune.

Now again this Branch to Aid Lithuania has donated eighty dollars to the cause of Lithuania's liberty. This sum has been received by the Lietuva, and will be sent to the Lithuanian Independence Fund. If all the Lithuanian colonies were as active, half of Lithuania's tears would be wiped away, and the prospect or Lithuania's independence would be twice as strong as it is now.
