Monday, Jan. 19, In Kauno, Lithuania, workers' blood was shed when Kauno sluggers sent bullets into the masses of working people. To remember this bloody Monday a mass meeting will be held Sunday, Feb. 22, at 2 P. M. All Chicago Lithuanian workers must be present.

Following is the advertisement inserted in the same pager. Vilnis, on February 20, 1925:

Mournful mass meeting or Remembrance of Kaunas Workers Slaughter, Sunday, Feb. 22, 1925, at M. Meldazis Hall., 2244 W. 23rd Place, at 1 P.M. Speakers Comrades R. Mizara, V. Andriulis, H. Jagminas, and a little girl from the "Budding Flower of Youth Society".

Mournful songs will be sung by the Lithuanian choir. A funeral march will be played by the International Workers' Orchestra. Other mournful melodies will be played by the Lithuanian Workers Student's Orchestra.

Comrades, workers, the heart breaking news has reached us. On January 2 19th the Lithuanian government shed workers' blood. On this day our rulers slaughtered three workers and injured over 30. Mounted police have trampled masses and cut them down with cattails; also people hidden in the churches were not overlooked. And what for? Just because hungry people went to the city hall to beg for a piece of bread. Instead of bread the self-elected government fed them with lead bullets. Three are killed, thirty injured and how many of the arrested will be sent to death?

Comrades, workers, can we neglect the Lithuanian workers? Can we keep quiet before this government of slaughterers? No! Let's gather all at this mournful mass meeting and vigorously protest against this self-elected government.

Invited by

American Workers Party

Lithuanian Section, 8th District.
