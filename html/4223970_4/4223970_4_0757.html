On the first day of this month Kazys Gugis was admitted to the federal court to practice law. The Chicago Lithuanian ought to be proud to have one more Lithuanian as attorney-at-law. We feel that all Lithuanians, not only from Chicago but from all over the United States, will congratulate our young attorney and wish him the best of luck in his new profession. In addition, attorney K. Gugis is very popular among the various Lithuanian organizations, and he is known as the most active individual in Chicago. He became well known among Lithuanian people while a student in the law school. He is a ceaseless worker and an organizer among our people in Chicago. For many years, as a member of the Socialist Party he supported the Socialist movement among Lithuanian people and helped to organize them.

He has been a very active member of the Socialist League in this country 2 for a good many years. As a member of the Lithuanian Alliance of America, the Lovers of Fatherland Society, and the Students Aid Society Aurora, he worked very hard to make them larger and stronger.

Recently he took an active part in the Chicago Lithuanian Society League, which consists of thirty different societies, and finally he became the president of this league. Moreover, he is a founder of Naujienos' corporation and has been elected president thereof. Attorney K. Gugis, with other Socialist leaders, realized the necessity of having Socialist newspapers in Chicago, which is the city with the largest Lithuanian colony in America. He recruited people for the Naujienos in his personal conversations, at various meetings, and by making personal contacts. He even went canvassing from house to house asking the people to subscribe to the Naujienos and to support the organization. He worked very hard until he accomplished his purpose. Of all Lithuanian public workers in Chicago, it is Attorney K. Gugis, who has helped most to establish the 3 Naujienos' corporation.

It is a remarkable thing that all this work was none by Attorney K. Gugis while he was a student in the law school. At the same time he was working his way through school.

This young attorney deserves all the credit that is given to him for taking active part among Lithuanian people in various Lithuanian organizations and attending the school of law at the same time. He showed unusual ambition for education and a strong desire for participating in various social circles.

It is not enough that he took an active part in various Lithuanian organizations, but he attended the Armour Institute of Technology, where he received a B.S. degree in mechanical engineering and then took up the study of law, graduating from Kent Law School this year and receiving his 4 degree. He set a very good example for the future Lithuanian students. He showed that if a man wants to accomplish something worthwhile in his life, he can do it with ease and become a professional in any field of education.

The working people should be proud to have such a man, bright and energetic, who comes from the working class and who is their leader and is fighting for their interests. When he came here from Lithuania he was very young and worked hard in the mines and steel mills, just like any other worker. He labored with others shoulder to shoulder and he did not feel that he was better than they. His attitude toward the working men has not changed. He still remains in their ranks as their leader. He has not forgotten his brother workers, even though he graduated from two colleges and has two degrees. Although a professional, he still feels that he is one of the people, struggling for existence.

Some people who attain degrees in higher education forget their brother workers, but attorney K. Gugis will never leave the ranks of the workers, and will never forget them, because he is one of them and will remain as a true member and their loyal leader. Friend K. Gugis, who is a brilliant speaker and an able man, will open his office and will start to practice law soon. We believe that he won't forget us when he starts to work in his new profession. He will support the ideas for which he fought while he was a student in college. We hope that he will remain an active member in our organization, a leader of the Socialist party among our people.
