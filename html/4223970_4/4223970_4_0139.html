When you read this letter cross yourself and pray for the everlasting rest of the soul of the soldier who was (lying when he gave me this paper. His name is James Furnance, a French Catholic. He left me all his wealth, but that wealth I did not keep for myself. I gave it to the poor and suffering Cubans. I just kept this piece of paper on which I am writing this letter.

I was wounded in the arm; the wound is not dangerous because the bullet just pierced the flesh and did not hit the bone. The soldiers who had been badly injured were taken to the United States. The doctor told me that in two or three months I will be all right and ready to join the fighting ranks again. For this reason they left me in Cuba.

At the front I met the Russian and the Polish officers. I recognized them by their uniforms, that they were from Russia. I want to them, shook hands, and talked with them in Russian. My officer thought that they were my relatives.

2

On June 22, the Cubans defeated the Spanish army at Morro Castle. The next day we landed on the shore and loaded our ammunition. On June 26, we came close to the city of Santiago. On July 1, we climbed the mountains to look over the city of Santiago in the valley. On July 14, we made an attack on the city; we had a hard fight, the rattling of the guns was so great that even the voice of the commanding officer was inaudible, so the officer gave us the orders by the shots of his revolver.

Then we were ordered to make the charge against the enemy. The fight was hard and severe. It would be hard to describe it. If I come back, I will tell more about this fight.

Sincerely,

Kaz. Biczus.
