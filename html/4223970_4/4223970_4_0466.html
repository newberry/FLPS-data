At the meeting of the St. Casimir Society held last Sunday in the St. George Parish Hall, as a result of a suggestion made by some good friends, one hundred dollars was donated from the treasury for the cause of Lithuanian independence. The money was sent to the Executive Committee of the Lithuanian Councils, in Washington, D. C.

The following were elected officers of the Society for 1919: V. Eringis, president, B. Grinius, vice-president, George Sireika, recording secretary, Joseph Palijanskas, financial secretary, and Joseph Ridikas, treasurer.

The St. Casimir Society is the oldest Lithuanian society in Chicago. It was founded more than thirty years ago. It has about two hundred members.
