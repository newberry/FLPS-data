The Simanas Daukantas Young People's Theatrical Club held a meeting at the Providence of God Parish Hall on December 7.

Having finished with the regular business of the organization, the secretary read a letter which had been sent to the Club by the Lithuanian National Fund. The letter asked for donations for the cause of Lithuanian liberty. After the letter was read, the members unanimously agreed to donate to the cause. One member suggested that the Club donate fifty dollars, but others thought that sum was too small, and insisted that the Club donate at least one hundred dollars. A motion was made and carried that the Club donate one hundred dollars. The vote was unanimous.

Though the donation is small, in comparison to the donations of some other 2 societies, still it is large for this Club. The Club has no money because it suffered a heavy financial loss last year when the bank in which it deposited its funds went bankrupt. Therefore, the members of the Club should be honored for making this donation, and for their love of and devotion to their native land. May this donation help to win freedom for Lithuania, and may the rays of that freedom brighten our lives forever.

While the officers of the Club were conversing after the meeting, the president, A. Brozis, who is a good patriot, said: "Men, we should feel ashamed to put the wages we receive as officers of the Club into our pockets, when Lithuania's pockets are emptier than ours. Therefore, wouldn't it be a good idea to donate this year's salary to the Fatherland?"

That is an exemplary suggestion which should be remembered at the next meeting when the officers will be paid their annual salaries. I believe that Mr. Brozis 3 will keep his word like a true and honorable patriot.

It was decided that the one-hundred dollar donation should be sent to the Executive Committee of the Lithuanian Councils in Washington, D. C.
