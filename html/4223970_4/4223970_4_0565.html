During the week of March 23, cablegrams were sent by two Chicago Lithuanian organizations to Petrograd, capital of Russia, to greet the new Government and the newly won liberty of Russia.

One of the cablegrams was sent by the Lithuania Club to Rodzianko, president of the Russian Duma. The cablegram reads as follows:

"Together with seventy-five thousand Chicago Lithuanians we are happy over the newly won liberty of Russia. Long live free and democratic Russia!"

The second cablegram was sent by Dr. Anthony J. Zimontas, president of the Lovers of the Fatherland Society [Translator's note: This society is also known in English as the Association of Lithuanian Patriots of America], in care of Prince Lvoff. The contents of the cablegram are as follows:

2

"On the morn of the birth of Russia's freedom the Association of Lithuanian Patriots of America begs Your Excellency to accept this expression or our profound joy and sympathy. Long live great Russia!"

The following message was received in answer to the above cablegram:

"Petrograd,

"April 1, 1917.

"President of the Association of Lithuanian Patriots, Chicago:

"Deeply touched by your telegram. I am convinced that the freedom of Lithuania will be established now that the freedom of all nations is an absolute and inalienable principle. Free Russia will do her duty for her Lithuanian brother.

"Prince Lvoff."

Prince Lvoff's telegram indicates that the Lithuanians who opposed the old Russian Government, but maintained friendly relations with the democratic people of Russia, did not make a mistake and will not be disappointed. As soon as the 3 democratic Russian people took over the Russian Government, their first step was to do away with the former government's persecution of other nationalities. Instead of persecuting and defrauding the oppressed nationalities, the new Government immediately declared that "the freedom of all nations is an absolute and inalienable principle," and for that reason, "the freedom of Lithuania will now be restored".
