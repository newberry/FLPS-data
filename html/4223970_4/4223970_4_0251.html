The said club held a banquet to help the revolution in Lithuania. The profit was $50.00. The money was sent to Mr. Duda, treasurer of the Central Revolutionary Committee.

It would be easy for other Lithuanian organizations to arrange such banquets, in order to raise money for the revolutionary cause in Lithuania. Everyone can understand from the appeals, that material help is very essential to the revolutionary movement in Lithuania.

We are sorry to say, that when we mailed tickets for this banquet to the societies in Chicago for them to sell and join us in helping the revolution, 2 we received complaints from some of the societies saying that they don't want to have anything to do with it. Some of the societies even told us that there is no revolution in Lithuania, and that the money does not reach the revolutionists, etc. Only two societies participated in this banquet, the Young American Lithuanian Pleasure Club and the King David Society.

We are not surprised, because the weather is cloudy in Chicago, and many people cannot see what is going on in Lithuania. Brothers, it is time to wipe the dust from your eyes, and help our brothers in Lithuania to throw off the paw of the Russian bear. Our brothers in Lithuania are shedding their blood and laying down their lives for the cause of freedom, but we still are doubting, hesitating to help them.

Committee
