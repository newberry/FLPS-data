A Lithuanian public mass meeting was held at the M. Ruzgis Hall, 45th and Marshfield Avenue, at 5 P. M., on November 26, 1905, to organize a committee to collect money for the revolutionary movement in Russia.

The meeting was opened by Mr. P. J. Varkala, who explained its purpose, asking the public to elect a chairman. Mr. Varkala was nominated and elected. In his speech, Mr. Varkala described the horrors of the Russian tsarist regime, against which the people of Russia and Lithuania have revolted.

The Russian revolution is going on; both the ignorant and the enlightened people have revolted against the tyranny of tsardom. The speaker appealed to the people to help the revolutionary movement in Russia Mr. J. Jancevskis and Mr. A. Algminavicia were the next speakers.

The Doctor Kudirka Singersâ€™ Society sang the Marseillaise. Then an 8-year-old girl, A. Kabasinskiute, recited the poem, "Liberty." The public was moved by this little girl's declamation. Then Dr. A. L. Graiciunas spoke, urging the people to contribute to the cause of the revolution. Some of the people started to leave the hall in order to avoid the donation. Then the doctor said, "Do not be degenerates. Do not blame us if when you go back to Lithuania the people will close the door against your face for refusing to help your brothers in their fight for freedom."

Other speakers were S. Marcinkevice, Golubickis, Bijanskis and Eismontas.

After the necessity of helping the revolution in Russia was discussed, there was elected a central committee to act for the revolutionary cause. Joseph Varkala, 4605 S. Ashland Avenue, was elected president; Stasys Marcinkevicia, 4605 S. Ashland Avenue, treasurer; Dr. A. L. Graiciunas, 3202 S. Halsted, secretary.

Authority was given to this central revolutionary committee to arrange 3 meetings all over Chicago to collect money and spread propaganda in behalf of the revolutionary movement in Russia, as well as to elect collectors with authority to go from house to house and get contributions for the revolutionary cause and for the orphans that were made during the revolution.

The following collectors were elected for the district of Town of Lake: Franciskus Poska, 4559 S. Hermitage Avenue; John Jankauskas, 4559 S. Hermitage Avenue; Mrs. Mary Damijonaitiene, 3108 S. Halsted St.; Peter Sliesoraitis, 4618 S. Paulina St.

The election of additional collectors was left to the central revolutionary committee.

At the massmeeting $19.25 was collected. This money was given to the treasurer, Mr. Stasys Marcinkevicia.
