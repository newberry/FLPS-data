Michael G. Valaska was born on November 5, 1872, and came to America on March 12, 1895, settling in Lawrence, Mass, where he worked in the textile mills. Then he came to Chicago (in 1900), where he worked in the Pullman car shops as a carpenter. In (his) spare time he worked in a printing shop, learning the printers' trade. In 1912 he established his own printing shop in Roseland, and is in the same business at present. During this time in business he has published seventeen Lithuanian books, both scientific and political.

In 1906 he published a monthly paper, the Coming World, and from 1921 to 1923 published a newspaper called Roselander. Both of these periodicals were monthly.

He belonged to many Lithuanian societies. At present he belongs to only two: The United Lithuanian Political Club and the Lithuanian Businessman's Association.

On May 28, 1904 he married Victoria Butenaite. They have one son, Algirdas Michael.

At present his printing business is located at 10819 S. Michigan Avenue, Chicago.
