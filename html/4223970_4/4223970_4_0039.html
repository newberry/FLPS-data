The good and honest man is not afraid, no matter what the people are saying about him, because the good deeds of a man are appreciated by all people. The honest man is not afraid of what the papers are writing and the people are talking about him, because nobody writes or says anything bad about a good man.

Look at the tsar of Russia. He upholds the severe censorship of books and newspapers because he wants to keep the people in subjection. The tsar is afraid of newspapers; his censors look over every newspaper before publication in order to see that nothing is written about the corruption and dissolute life of his court and among the tsar's officials. As long as the people are ignorant they believe that the tsar is divine and that God put the golden crown on his brainless head. It is very easy to exploit the ignorant people, and to keep them in subjection. The people without newspapers are blind, they blindly believe and obediently fulfill oven the worst orders of the tsar.

What the tsar is doing with censorship of newspapers in Russia is the same thing being done by our Rev. Krawczunas in America. The priest is more afraid of liberal newspapers than the tsar because the newspapers are bringing into the light the bad manners of the priest, and sharing that the priests conduct is no different from the tsar's low corrupted life. And because the newspapers are bringing into the light the priest's corruption, the priest wants to destroy such newspapers by threatening the people with hell in order to keep them from reading the newspapers that are telling the truth. Our Rev. Krawczunas warned the people many times not to read our newspaper. On St. Bartholomew Day, in Waukegan, the priest told the people during the sermon that it was a deadly sin to read tha newspaper Lietuva. Such a sermon of the priest and the denunciation of our newspaper, as we have stated in previous numbers of our paper, brought bad results for the priest himself, because we received from Waukegan thirteen new subscriptions to our paper after the priest's "great" sermon.

In Chicago. Rev. Krawczunas does not have enough courage to denounce our newspaper, so he told his assistant, Vicar Steponaviezius, to execrate the newspaper which does not uphold the Rev. Krawczunas' aim. So the Vicar Steponaviezius, in his Sunday sermon, September 11, told the parishioners as follows: "The godless newspaper accused our rector; found out that there was no God nor the Blessed Lady. From this day on every one will be questioned at the confessional about what papers he reads, and whoever reads the godless newspaper will get no absolution.

Priest, you are lying! Even you did not mention the newspaper's name. We understand about which newspaper you were talking. Our newspaper knows God better than you with your rector! If you and your rector respected God, then you would not lie in the church, in the house of God; you would not curse as if you were in a saloon, but you would propagate the glory of God and love among the people. If Rev. Krawczunas respected God, he would have not sold the house of God, the church and 4 all the properties, without the parishionersâ€™ knowledge for $15,000 to the Milwaukee Bank, like Judah sold Lord Jesus! Why, Judah himself, when he kissed Christ, pretended that he was kissing Him from love, but he kissed Him in order to show the Jews which one He was, and then he sold Him to them for thirty cents. In our church, the priest, instead of preaching the word of God, is preaching lies and blinding the people with his lies.

Thousands of people are reading our newspaper; let, then, even one person say when our newspaper said that there was no God or no Blessed Mother. Why, the priest is not ashamed to lie in the eyes of all the people in the church, before the altar of God! Our newspaper did not say that there was no God, but said that there was no money in the church treasury. We did not ask where God vanished. but what became of the money that the people deposited with Rev. Krawczunas. Is money the god of the priest? Where is the morality among our priests? Brothers, what benefit can we 5 get from such priests who, in the house of God, are feeding the people with lies?

Now you understand why some of the priests are forbidding the people to read the newspapers. The priests know that the newspapers are telling the people the truth, and that the truth is not afraid of light; that the people will find out that our priests are not right. Therefore, the priests are afraid that the people may learn from the newspapers about their bad conduct. The priests know that the people, by reading newspapers, will learn about their hypocrisy and will force the priests to improve themselves. If some of the priests would not improve themselves and would refuse to fulfill God's order, then, as wrongdoers. they shall be chased out from the people, shall be rooted out like the kernel from the wheat.

Our priest have seen himself that the people know that he is lying, because 6 as soon as the priest starts to denounce the newspaper in the pulpit, the people leave the church right away. The people do not want to listen to the priest's lies. Our Rev. Krawczunas convinced himself that with his own words he is unable to fool the people. Now he wants to establish his own newspaper. Well, he may have better success with a newspaper to obtain his aim. That Rev. Krawczunas really wants to publish a newspaper we have seen in Nos. 73 and 74 of the newspaper Saule (The Sun), published in Mahanoy City, Pa.

We are willing to warn our priest that on this newspaper which he wants to publish in order to defend his personal affairs he should not take the money from the parish budget because sooner or later he will be forced to make account of that money. In order to avoid trouble we are asking the priest to buy one raffle ticket for $1.50 of the Polish newspaper Nowe Zvoie (New Life). The publisher of this newspaper is selling the machinery and the newspaper on raffle tickets. We have no doubt that God will help our priest. Therefore, he can get the newspaper and all the machinery for $1.50.

For all of your requirements the salary is not enough for you, and from the parish treasury you cannot take handfuls of money anymore. And in the end, even the Lithuanians will become disgusted with such a benefactor who has only one intention in his life and that is how to extort as much money as possible in various schemes from the people.

Therefore, we are heartily advising to start to work among the Poles because the Poles do not demand so much morality of their priests as the Lithuanians do. Many such immoral priests that have been abused by Lithuanians found refuge among the Poles. And our priest is the first on the list now.

If you withdraw on your own accord from Lithuanians, you will do a great benefit to them. Try, then you will see that for such a sacrifice to the Lithuanian benefit, all of us will praise you. What will our priest choose to do now?
