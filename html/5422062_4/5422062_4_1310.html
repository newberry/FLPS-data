Last Sunday, in their special headquarters, the Greek Liberals of Chicago held their annual election of executive officers.

The committee assigned to preside over the election was composed of Messrs. K. Paleologos, A. Baxevani, B. Biniari, and P. Prima.

The satisfactory conduct of the electoral campaign was due to the efforts of the retiring president of the organization, Mr. Constantine Paleologos.

At the election preceding, 450 members and been admitted to the polls, whereas at this election 1,015 members were permitted to vote. About 731 new members have been enrolled.

The election began at 9 a.m. and ended at 9 p.m., with an intermission of forty-five minutes in the afternoon.

The majority of the members of the organization were more than satisfied by the elections of Mr. Demos, the new president, and his advisory committee.

The truth is that Mr. Demos and the rest of the candidates elected have shown themselves, by their manners and their other qualifications, to be very progressive individuals.

We congratulate the victors, and we wish them the best of everything.
