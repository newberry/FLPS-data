p. 5.- The social life of our Chicago community has slowly lessened and is practically at a stand still now. It is not because the winter season, which is the peak of social affairs, is giving away to spring, but because everyone is taking time out to go to church.

The Tessarakosti has finally come and it reminds us that Easter is not far off. This is the one time of the year when most Greeks remember their religious duties and hurry to the church to become Christians again.
