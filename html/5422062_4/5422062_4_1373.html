Macaulay once said, "The hearts of men are their books; events are their tutors; great actions are their eloquence." We want every single Greek to read this and think about it. Could this philosophy apply to us or to our societies?

We all know the answer. Take, for example, the recent attempt to establish a Panhellenic Union in Chicago. There is plenty of eloquence but where came find action? Amongst themselves the leaders are making a very bad mess of things. This is not as it should be.

Let us try to remember Macaulauy's saying and profit by its meaning.
