Philip Newburgh was born in Sulzberg, Bavaria, February 6, 1813. He came to the United States in 1837. In 1841, he came to Chicago with his family, where his daughter Pauline was born. She appears to have been the first Jewish child born in Chicago.

Louis Newburgh, who was born in Pennsylvania two years prior to the family's arrival here, was the first to become Bar Mitzvah in Chicago in 1851.

A third child, Henry, born in 1846 is from all accounts the first Jewish male child born in Chicago.
