One hundred and eighty-five Poles attended and benefited from the lectures on citizenship conducted at the Northwestern University Settlement and Pulaski Park.

Besides that thirty-two applications of Poles were written out for first citizenship papers. The course lasted from September 15th to December 7th, 1921, and lectures took place every Monday and Thursday.

The present course will begin January 5th at 7:30 P. M. at the Northwestern University Settlement, Noble and Augusta Streets, and in Pulaski Park on Monday, January 9th, at 7:30 P. M.

These lectures will be conducted for the convenience of the Poles in the Polish and English language. The lectures will continue to be conducted by Mr. Joseph Gagol.

For further information apply every Monday evening at Pulaski Park at Noble and Blackhawk Street, or on Thursdays at Northwestern University Settlement at Noble and Augusta Street.

Miss Vittum

Supervisor

N. U. Settlement

F. Johnson, Manager

Pulaski Park.
