Notice about the opening on November 1, 1926, of a new Russian school at 2127 Crystal Street, for children from seven to fourteen years old. The school was founded by the Russian Center of Chicago.

Classes in the Russian language and Russian literature were held twice a week. The teacher was Mr. V. I. Pustovoitov, a Russian engineer.

Note: This school is extinct. It lasted only a short time. D. S.
