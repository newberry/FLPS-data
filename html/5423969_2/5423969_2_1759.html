Last week, the Norsemen's Singing Society gave an affair in honor of its new director, Gustav Carlson; Karl M. Hagland acted as chairman. Elmer J. Johnson gave an address in honor of the new director. I. W. Colberg spoke at length about the future of the Society. He stressed very sharply the need of unity and tolerance within the society.

The singers sang several songs, first under the direction of Colberg, and later under the direction of the new leader, Carlson.
