With the large number of Scandinavian residents in Chicago, it was inevitable that a demand would arise for the establishing of Scandinavian banks. The State Bank of Chicago was first known as the Haugan and Lindgren Bank. H. A. Haugan, a native of Norway, and the Swede, John R. Lindgren, organized the State Bank of Chicago. Associated with them in the management of the Bank were A. P. Johnson, A. Jurgens, P. S. Peterson, and, later, J. Carlson.
