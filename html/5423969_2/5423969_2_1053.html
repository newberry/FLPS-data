Jacob Johnson, born in Norway, came to America while yet a boy and settled in Chicago. He was prominent in the lumber trade for forty years and became part owner of the schooners, Dolphin and E. M. Portch. The following years he purchased the schooner, Cal. H. C. Heg. Later he became part owner of the schooners, T. Y. Avery, Whirlwind, Tempest, North Cape, and the tug, Mc Clelland, and sole owner of the tugs, C. W. Parker, W. H. Wolf, and L. B. Johnson.
