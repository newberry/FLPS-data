February 13, 1923.

P. S. Lambros, Esq.,

The Greek Star,

Chicago, Illinois.

My dear Mr. Lambros:

As a lover of Lincoln and a student of Pericles I was greatly interested to see in today's New York paper a brief account of your address before the Hamilton Club on the occasion of the celebration of Lincoln's birthday yesterday.

I wish to congratulate you. The full text of your address was not printed, but enough appeared to indicate how you compared Lincoln's Gettysburg address with Pericles's funeral oration, and I gathered also that you made a comparison between the State of the Union at the time of Lincoln's \2famous speech and conditions in Greece in the years of the Civil War among the Greek states when Pericles lived.

It would have been a pleasure to me to read the address in full.

With kind regards,

Cordially yours,

Fr. W. Jackson, attorney,

111 Broadway, New York City.
