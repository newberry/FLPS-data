The Ceskoslovanský Pomocný Výbor (Czechoslovak Aid Committee) held a meeting in the Pilsen Sokol hall yesterday at which they welcomed Vladimir E. Scolba, first lieutenant in the Serbian Army. He came to Chicago with the Serbian delegates and has stayed here for some time to recruit Serbians, Croatians, and Slovenians for the Serbian Army, which is in Macedonia now. These men will follow a contingent of two thousand men which left for the front recently.

Mr. Scolba paid a visit to the Czechs to thank them for the good they have done for his poor country. He also conveyed greetings from the Serbian soldiers and told about his experiences in the war.
