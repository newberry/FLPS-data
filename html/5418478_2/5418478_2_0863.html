It is constantly being recommended to the people that they refrain from eating meat on two days each week. This is based on the fact that the measure would bring about lower food prices. If only Congress would set aside two days for sessions to act, and not to talk, there would be a great improvement. The people would await the arrival of food control and other important laws more patiently.

Meatless days will arrive, however, long before the moment when congressmen have grasped the fact that they are sent to Washington not to excel exclusively in talking.
