The thirteenth convention of the Cesko-Slovanska Bratrska Podporující Jednota (Bohemian-Slavonic Fraternal Benevolent Society) came to a close yesterday. The last session was a very busy one and lasted until 6:30 P. M. Many important decisions were made which will no doubt be of considerable benefit to the members. This applies, in the first place, to a new table of rates....and to the body's own organ, whose publication was agreed upon.....

Chicago was selected as the seat of the headquarters until the next convention, which will be held in Pittsburgh in five years.

....Also, at yesterday's session, donations were made to various national institutions. The Cesko-Americka Matice Skolska (Bohemian-American Scholastic Association) received fifty dollars; the Sbor Zastupcu Ceske Skoly (Board of Bohemian School Representatives) in New York, the Sdruzeni Svobodomyslnych 2Skol (Federation of Bohemian Free Thought Schools) in Cleveland, and the Bohemian schools in Baltimore, twenty-five each. The schools in Cedar Rapids, Iowa; Little Ferry, New Jersey; Newark, New Jersey; Union Hill, New York; Astoria, Long Island, New York; Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania; Loyalhanna, Pennsylvania; Mt. Pleasant, Pennsylvania; Boston, Massachussetts; and Buffalo, New York, received ten dollars each. The Spolek Lidumil (Society Lidumil) in New York, fifty dollars; the Machar Library in Chicago, ten dollars; the Cesko-Americka Tiskova Kancelar (Bohemian-American Press Bureau) in Chicago, twenty-five dollars.....

The salaries of the officers of the Grand Lodge were raised as follows: President, from $15 to $25; secretary, from $600 to $700; treasurer, from $100 to $180. The salary of the editor of the organ was set at $180 a year.....
