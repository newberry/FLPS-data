The State Supreme Court yesterday rejected the appeal of Arthur Lorenz. He is the former editor-in-chief of the Illinois Staats-Zeitung, and was sentenced by Judge Pam to six months in the workhouse and fined one dollar for criminal libel directed against the American Legion.

In his defense plea, he pointed out that he had not attacked any specific person. In its decision handed down yesterday, the court of highest appeal argued that libeling a class or a group has the same tendency to incite to a breach of the peace or to inflame the populace as the libeling of an individual. Such a libel is punishable even if it could be proved that it was not intended for individuals of the class or group in question.

Judge Heard handed down a dissenting opinion.
