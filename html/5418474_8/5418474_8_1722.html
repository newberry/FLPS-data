The German Society,which began its benevolent activities fifty-five years ago last March, reported a slight improvement in the deplorable conditions prevalent during a period of four years. However, less employment could be obtained for those seeking work.

During the month of March, the German Society, despite almost exhausted funds, assisted fifty-three families with a total of 164 children, and also helped twenty-three single persons. It provided shelter and food for 371 persons; it arranged for the hospitalization of five persons in privately owned hospitals and of seven persons in Cook County Hospital; and it made arrangements for five people to become residents of the Oak Forest Infirmary. In addition, the Society secured free medical treatment for thirteen patients, etc. The total cost of assistance rendered by the German Society rose to $709.97.
