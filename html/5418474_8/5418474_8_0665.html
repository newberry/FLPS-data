When it can be said of any newspaper, that it answers a necessity, it corresponds to the monthly magazine, "The Modern Journal" for German ladies", edited and published in Milwaukee by the Germania Magazine Company, Germania Building.

There have been German newspapers for years in this country, which have been published for certain classes, clerical or otherwise, and have fulfilled their objective in a very satisfactory manner. But as a German-American family magazine the Modern Journal takes a unique position in the newspaper market. Its contents are superlatively good and especially suitable for German Americans. As regards pictures and other illustrations it can measure with the best magazines in existence of English-American enterprise. Each number has twenty-eight pages and costs .10 annual subscription only. 50c. It competes well with all German imported magazines and deserves the best success.
