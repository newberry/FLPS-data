The Young club seems to prosper splendidly. Its plans. In the Bismarck Hotel there there was held yesterday afternoon, the first regular meeting of the branch "National Union of German-American Journalists and Authors." Thirty new members were admitted, among them almost all of the representatives of German elements of the different judicial staffs. There are more than eighty members.

The board was instructed to regulate the question of a permanent club site as soon as possible, and, thereby, to create a central meeting place for club members, their ladies and friends. The president was empowered, to appoint members for an entertainment committee, who should at once take steps and make plans to arrange private and public programs.
