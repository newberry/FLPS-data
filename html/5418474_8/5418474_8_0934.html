"What help was it to Louis the Sixteenth and his wife," said he, "that they were the most virtuous royal couple, who in that century stood at the head of France, that at heart they wanted to do a lot of good, although they were hindered by their surroundings and conditions in carrying it out?

"When they ascended the throne, the measure of revolt and exasperation of the people was already full, and it only needed a trifle, a small incentive, to make it overflow. With their lives and blood did they have to expiate the sins which their predecessors on the throne, the whole court and the aristocracy of the country, committed against the people, against nature and against virtue.
