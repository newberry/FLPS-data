At yesterday's directors meeting a sum of $100 was voted upon by the directors of the Polish Union Daily, in the thought of the appeal placed in the paper a few days ago in regard to offerings for the former unemployed strikers.

Labor affairs have always been discussed in the columns of our paper and the workingmen have been convinced that through their conviction we are guarding their cause

We have not desired and do not want to demonstrate a feigned friendship, as others are doing, but will always stand up and guard their interests.

2

The Polish Union Daily employs union labor and pays the union scale. We did not play as demagogues of the type of a doctrinaire editor of a non-union newspaper, but wrote sincerely, caring about the fate of Polish workers, exploited by an unfair capitalist and in man cases a corrupt union official.

We appeal to our more well-to-do readers to send donations for this cause, and all donations will be turned over to the Welfare Societies of the Polish parishes in the Town of Lake district.
