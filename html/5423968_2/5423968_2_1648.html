In order to avert further conditions of poverty among the Stockyards' strikers, Dennis Lane, secretary of the Amalgamated Meat Cutters and Butcher Workers (sic), proposed to the company officials that they arrange a vote of the workers to find out whether or not they want to return to work. The executives informed him that no strike exists in their concerns and declined any further negotiations.

The United States Department of Labor made an attempt to barter with packing house heads in having an arbitrator settle the differences, but nothing was accomplished.
