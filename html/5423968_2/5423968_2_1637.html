There has been no decided change in employment during the west few days in the Stockyards. Hundreds of workers have returned to ask for their jobs but only a comparative few were hired. Hundreds are still out of work because the strikebreakers occupy their places.

In the Town of Lake and Bridgeport families are found penniless because of the strike. Many are still conking for aid to the union headquarters at 48th and Paulina Streets; however, the numbers have decreased somewhat. But still there are many in dire need.
