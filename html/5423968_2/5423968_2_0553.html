"Orphan asylums have almost everything else but orphans in them these days", declares the Children's Dureau of the Department of Labor after completing a survey of these institutions.

Information gathered from nine states shows that out of a total of nearly twenty thousand children, wholly or partially dependent on public aid, only five percent were real orphans, while sixty percent five percent had both parents living.

Investigation showed that separation and divorce were by far the greatest menace to the welfare of minors.
