On Thursday evening a conference of the St. Louis Aid Committee with the Chicago Germans took place in the Congregational Church at Ann and Washington Street, in order to organize the mass emigration of work-seeking unemployed and indigent. The St. Louis Committee promised they will be comfortably settled and arrange for free passage. A provisory General Committee was formed of the following gentlemen, who will seek out in the various parts of the city, those Germans (single, and families) who want to change to St. Louis: B. Lowenthal, Julius Rosenthal, Dr. Ernst Schmidt, Jonas, Th. Schintz, Schaffner, F. Busse, A. Busse, Michelson, H. Beck, Knauer, T. H. Richberg etc.
