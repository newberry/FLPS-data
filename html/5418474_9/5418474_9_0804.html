Mr. Adolf Frese, organizer of the German Charity League, which was founded 16 years ago in Pittsburgh, organized lately two lodges in Chicago. The establishment of the new lodges, which will bear the numbers 334 and 335, will be celebrated at a meeting tonight in Sieben's Hall, 174 Clybourn Avenue, and two more high officials of the League from Pittsburgh, Messrs. Louis Voltz, Supreme President and Joseph Klaus, Supreme Treasurer are going to be present.

The League has, since its existence of 16 years, spread over ten states, with a membership of 14 thousand. Besides the work of assisting needy ones, it has made it as its task, to cultivate the German language, German customs and habits and participates in the fight against bigots.
