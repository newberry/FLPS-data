According to a report given by the business manager of the German Society, this organization has spent during the month of October a total of $396.15. They supported 94 families with 282 children and 50 single persons. In 225 cases board and lodging was given.

For 562 persons jobs were obtained which is quite an accomplishment for this time of the year. Most of them found work on farms. The demand for factory laborers was extremely low. About this time of the year many of the unemployed from other cities come to Chicago and therefore the increased number of those needing help.

Members paid a total of $670.50 in fees.

Used clothing and shoes will be gladly accepted and distributed to poor German families.
