In all the grades of the public schools where German is taught, 26,801 pupils availed themselves of the course, according to the September census; and during that period there were 40,796 scholars registered, which shows that 66 per cent, or two thirds of the total number, studied the Foreign language.

Of the 26,801 pupils, 12,268 were of German origin and 7,773 of Anglo-American parcentage, while 6,760 came of IRISH, Scandinavian, Bohemian, etc., ancestry. German instruction is given by 191 teachers, all women.
