The Central Committee of the Socialist Labor Party met yesterday evening at 54 W. Lake Street. Eric Johnson presided. After attending to current business matters, Ald. Christ Meiser was requested to present the resolutions he read in the convention, which contained accusations against the "Arbeiter Zeitung" and Grottkau. After a lengthy debate, it was found, that the accusations were not well founded and Comrade Christ Meiser was instructed to put them in writing; also to submit the necessary proof to a Special commission of inquiry.-

Comrade August Kelting was elected Treasurer in place of Hoerigs. Comrade Voigt declined his nomination as Senator of the 6th district for the reason that he could not qualify, because he did not reside in the State long enough.

After this the meeting was adjourned.
