We are now living in the year 1909. But when the State Bank of Chicago (Haugan and Lindgren's Bank) was established they wrote 1879 on the date line.

This bank has thus been in operation for thirty years, during which time it has served Chicago's Scandinavian public with honesty and efficiency, and won its confidence and good will.

The bank is capitalized at one million dollars and its reserves have now reached $1,300,000. It is thus the largest Scandinavian bank in America.

Three per cent interest is paid on savings accounts.
