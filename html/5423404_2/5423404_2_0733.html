Our distinguished countryman, Architect Lawrence G. Hallberg, observed his sixty-fifth birthday on September 4. He was born in Nas, Vester-Gotland in 1844, and graduated from the Polytechnical school of Gothenburg, whereupon he attended the Institute of Free Arts in Stockholm, later practicing his profession in Gefle.

In 1871 he came to Chicago where he has become prominent in architectual circles. His offices are at 84 South La Salle Street, and his home is in Evanston.
