Added proof of the fact that the enthusiasm for the cause of the Balkan Slavs, who are engaged in a death struggle with their oppressors, is not diminishing among us was demonstrated at yesterday's spirited meeting held. Under the auspices of the Cesko-Americka Narodni Rada (Bohemian-American National Council) in the spacious pavilion of the Pilsen Brewing Company.

Like the Pilsen Auditorium two weeks ago, the pavilion of the Pilsen Brewing Company attracted a huge throng of men who were led by a single idea: To offer their sympathy and their money to aid a cause which is common to all of us--the liberation of the Slavs.

Those gathered in yesterday's assembly were mostly Bohemians; but just the same, we saw quite a number of Yugoslavs both in the audience and on the speakers' platform.....

The chairman of yesterday's meeting was the Clerk of the Probate Court, Mr. John A. Cervenka....who introduced the first speaker of the day, Mr. Jar. K. Kosar.....

His words provoked stormy applause when he said that Mr. Stepina has so far received $2,200 in contributions for the Slavic Red Cross. The money was sent to Professor Pupin in New York.....

The envelope collection yielded $408.15......

Contributions for the Slavic cause are being solicited everywhere.....As far as our Sokols are concerned, the Narodni Jednota Sokolska (National Sokol Unity) is getting ready to inaugurate a fifty-cent head tax, which, computing its membership at about seven thousand, would mean a substantial increase in 3the general fund. A similar step is contemplated by the Sokolska Zupa Fuegner-Tyrs (Fuegner-Tyrs Sokol Division).....Why could not each of our building and loan associations declare an extra installment?
