On Sunday, April 18, at 1 p.m. sharp, Mr. Sabinsky, president of the Independent Society, appeared on the platform of the hall of the People's School on Wood street and greeted the delegates who had gathered in order to hold the Convention.

The Convention was attended by 40 delegates from ten branches of the Society.

After the words of welcome said by the president, the secretary of the Society, Mr. Dzaiko, read the half-yearly report.

During the last six months the receipts of the Society exceeded the sum of $9,000; the expenditure for the same period of time was $8,000 which sum has been spent in the following way: over $7,000 was given out in benefits paid to the families of deceased members of the Society. Each family has received $800.

The salaries paid to the president, the secretary and the treasurer amounted 2 to $105.

Expenditure for office supplies--$17, etc.

After the reading of this report the Revising Committee submitted its report.

In this report it was pointed out that the Society had grown larger and stronger; that the state of the treasury was satisfactory; that the accounts were kept carefully and the bookkeeping was accurate.

Finally the members of the Committee of the Society were thanked for the honest and untiring work done by them....

G. Gorsky.
