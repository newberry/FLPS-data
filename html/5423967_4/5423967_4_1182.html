On June 5 and 6, 1937, the Russian Evangelical Christians of Chicago were celebrating the 25th anniversary of the foundation of their community in this city. There were solemn services at the church Saturday evening and Sunday morning and evening. Representatives of the Russian Evangelical communities of Detroit, Albion, Mlch.; New York, Kenosha, St. Paul, Minneapolis and Beaver Dam, Mich. had come to the celebration.

The former preacher, Rev. J. Johnson, also came from New York.

On Sunday all the members and guests were invited to a fraternal banquet, which was attended by more than 200 persons.
