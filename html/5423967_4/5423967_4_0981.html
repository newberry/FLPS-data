I. Name of the Society:

1. National Russian Christian Students' Society of the City of Chicago.

II. Objects of the Society:

2. Development of the spirit of mutual aid and friendship; culture of Christian feelings; improvement of the spiritual and moral qualities of the members of the Society.

3. The accumulation of knowledge and information which can be useful to Russia in the future.

4. The origination and establishment in the future of a peaceful cooperation of the two great nations inhabiting Russia and the United States of North America.

5. Material aid to those students who are full fledged members of the society, enabling them to complete their higher education.

6. The Society is a non-political organization.

III. The Area of Activity of the Society:

7. The Society functions in the region of the following states: 1) Illinois, 2) Michigan, 3) Indiana, 4) Missouri, 5) Arkansas, 6) Louisiana, 7) Texas, 8) Oklahoma, 9) Kansas, 10) Nebraska, 11) South Dakota, 12) North Dakota, 13) Iowa, 14) Wisconsin, and 15) Minnesota. The headquarters of the society are in Chicago.

IV. The Rights of the Society:

22. The Society has the right: (a) To open mutual benefit societies consisting of members of the society and functioning under rules specially established by the General Assembly.

(b) To amalgamate with Russian Students' Unions existing in the United States.

(c) To enter into agreements and relations with other societies and cooperatives in order to undertake together some activities.

(d) To have a community house and a dining room.

(e) To open [gap] (Note: Probably some kind of committee. I do not know what this word means. I never have heard that word in Russia. It is evidently derived from starosta, an elder. D. S.) in those neighborhoods where the Society is functioning.

(f) To have its own press.
