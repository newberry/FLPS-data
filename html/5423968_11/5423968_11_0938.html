In behalf of the great honor bestowed upon a Polish priest who will in a few days be consecrated the first Polish bishop in America, the following rhyme was written by a Polish citizen:

Hail youthful prelate, son of the fairland

That to the world a Sobieski gave;

From Islam's power Christendom to save.

Where Kosciuszko and his gallant band

For freedom's cause made memorable stand;

And prince and priest and peasant, true and brave,

Unflinching faced the dungeon or the grave

Rather than tamely bear the helot's brand

'Tis meet a prelate boasting Irish blood

Shall give to thee the consecrating rite,

For, like fair Poland, Ireland aye has stood

For faith and country foremost in the fight

So, ever linked, may Celt and Pole be true

To God and home as symbolized in you.
