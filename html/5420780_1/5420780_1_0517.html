We wonder whether there is a single normally minded individual in all the U. S. who will deny the fact that big business, trusts and monopolies are flourishing, if not actually dominating the nation, worse than even five or ten years ago.

In spite of that, thousands upon thousands of people will stand up and cheer as soon as a man like President Roosevelt boasts of his fight against these same trusts and monopolies and declares that he will continue to fight them.

A person would actually think that Abraham Lincoln was wrong when he said that "nobody can fool all the people all the time."
