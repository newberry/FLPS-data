Wm. Johnson was the first Chicago alderman elected on a straight socialistic ticket. He was born at Kristiania, Norway in 1875. He came to this country in 1888. He belongs to the Wood Carvers Union and works at the Pullman Shops. He is still unmarried and has a large library, his motto is: "Drink less beer, and buy more books". He was elected by the Scandinavian votes. He is not much of a speaker, but he is well known in the Danish societies, where he is well liked because of his fine personality.
