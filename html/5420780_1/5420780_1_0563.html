President Roosevelt's uncalled for condemnation of the two labor leaders held in Idaho on a criminal charge (but not convicted) has caused so much indignation that the President himself found it appropriate to come forward with an explanation which, by the way, did anything but justify his statement. By arguing that the workers all over the country have taken sides with the accused men, he tries to hide his own mistake of joining their accusers.
