The editor tells us that Victor L. Berger proposed three resolutions at the last convention of the American Federation of Labor, in San Francisco: that the Federation adopt the principles of socialism; that, instead of a professional army, America should have a people's army like that in Switzerland; and that old workers should be pensioned when they retire from active work. All three resolutions were voted down. The editor believes that Gompers, Mitchell and Company are doing a fine job for the American capitalists.
