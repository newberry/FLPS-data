The census of 1910 showed that there was 181,000 Danes in the United States. 147,000 children were born here with both parents Danish. About 72,000 children had one of its parents Danish.

To this number must be added the great number of South Jutlanders, who in the census are counted as Germans, but in fact are the most ardent Danes anywhere. The number of Danes in U.S.A. is therefore more than 225,000.

The figures for Illinois are: 15,684 Danes in 1900 and 17,368 Danes in 1910. The percentage of Danes as to the entire population is 1.4% in Illinois. 11,551 children had both parents Danish. 4,600 had one parent Danish.
