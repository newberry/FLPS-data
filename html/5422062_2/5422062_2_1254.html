Young Theodore Mouzakeotis, who expects to receive his diploma from the Illinois Medical School next June, has already achieved a highly coveted goal. He was fortunate enough to pass the difficult examinations of the County Hospital and to be accepted as an interne.

He is the first Greek to be awarded an internship in Cook County Hospital. The examinations were taken by two hundred and sixty, and only sixty-five passed.

Gaining an internship in this hospital is the dream of every medical student, because of the opportunity it presents for study, and for contact with rare diseases and great surgical achievements. It is a position that can be 2obtained only through proven ability.
