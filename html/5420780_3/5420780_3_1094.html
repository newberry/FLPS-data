Professor Jens Iversen Westengaard, AL.B. and A.M. of Harvard University, left last week for Bangkok, the capitol of Siam, where for a year he is going to be the legal adviser to the king of Siam. He has a one-year leave of absence from the Harvard School of Law. At the end of last semester the degree of Master of Arts, honoris causa, was conferred upon him. This is the first time a degree of this kind has ever been given to a Scandinavian in the United States.

Professor J. I. Westengaard is a young man. His parents are Danes, still living in Chicago. He writes and speaks Danish fluently, and has visited Denmark twice.
