Born at Ilskov, Herning, Feb. 12, 1849, served apprenticeship as watchmaker from 1864 to 1866. Went to Hamburg and learned to be a machinist, became an expert maker of knitting machines, and was later on a manufacturer of such machines for 10 years in Copenhagen.

In 1889 he came to Chicago and started, in 1892, a knitting factory with two machines; in 1920 he had a hundred. He sold out in 1928.

To his wife, an expert knitter, Mr. Jensen gives all credit for his great financial success in business. He is a member and ex-president of Dania Society. Now lives at 2430 N. Lowell.
