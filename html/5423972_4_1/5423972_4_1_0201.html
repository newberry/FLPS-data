Henry G. Foreman celebrated his seventieth birthday during the past week. He was born in Chicago at Madison and Wells street.

Mr. Foreman was appointed a south Park commissioner in 1902 and was president of the Board from 1903 to 1913. He was instrumental in developing the present system of small parks in Chicago. Under his guidance the South Park Board instituted a series of fourteen small parks.

Mr. Foreman was elected president of the County Board in 1902 and during his regime originated and carried out many improvements. He was one of the early leaders in the movement for the forest preserves.
