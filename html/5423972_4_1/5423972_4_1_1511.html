He (Henry Greenebaum) came to Chicago, so he replied to Mr. Swett, (lawyer for the defense), in 1849, and has banked here from 1854 to 1877, doing a private business until 1870, and then organizing the German National. That bank started in January, 1871, with $250,000 capital, which was increased during the year to $500,000. He was president of that bank, also of the German Savings Bank.
