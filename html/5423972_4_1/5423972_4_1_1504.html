Mr. Joseph Brunswick, one of Chicago's oldest citizens, departed this life Sunday, 5:30 A. M., at his residence, 1252 Prairie avenue. He was born March 20, 1823 at Langnan, Switzerland, coming to this country in 1851, where at Cincinnati, in company with an elder brother, the foundation of the quat biliard table industry was instituted. After a partnership of nineteen years, it was dissolved Joseph Brunswick coming to Chicago an engaging in the same business at 57 State street, in company with two younger brothers. The great fire destroyed their establishment and it was rebuilt on Michigan avenue, with others admitted to the firm. The business grew until it extended to the principal Western cities.

Rabbi Hirsch conducted the funeral services.
