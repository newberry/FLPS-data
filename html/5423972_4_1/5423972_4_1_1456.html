Today the departed Henry Greenbaum will be brought to his everlasting rest in Rosehill. The corpse will be brought to Sinai Temple from where, after a eulogy by Rabbi Hirsch, the funeral procession will wend its way to the cemetery.

The deceased was not only one of the founders of the first synagogues in Chicago, but also one of the first founders of the Sefran Isaiah, the Sinai Temple, the United Hebrew Charities, the Order B'Nai B'rith, and many other societies, among them some of Christian nature. As a lover of music, he helped financially many musical talents.

In recent years he was connected with the Equitable Life Insurance Company. He was also the founder of two large banks.
