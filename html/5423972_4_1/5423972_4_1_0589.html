The Chicago Jewry was bitterly provoked by a report published in the Chicago Daily News which throws doubt upon the patriotism of the Chicago Jews.

According to reports, it was revealed that the local office of the Justice Department summoned Election Commissioner Arthur Luders to appear before a hearing for having uttered unpatriotic expressions about the question of inviting the French Commission to Chicago.

Mr. Luder is said to to have rebuked Alderman McNuckles, of the 10th Ward, for signing a petition which aimed to call a special meeting of the City Council about inviting the French Commission and Marshall Joffre.

Luders does not deny this, but when asked why he said it, he answered: 2 "Because McNuckles will soon be a candidate for re-election, and the Jews of his ward disapprove of the whole matter." As soon as the news scattered throughout the city, a great number of Jews called the Courier, asking the editor to express his sharpest protest against Luder's assertion.

The Jews are patriotic and devoted, body and soul, to the United States Government and are supporting it, in all possible ways in the present war for Democracy against Autocracy.
