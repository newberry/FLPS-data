At yesterday's concluding session of the Industrial Relations Commission, Dr. Sachs of the Chicago Tuberculosis Institute, as well as other prominent men of the industrial world, spoke.

The Jewish doctor depicted the work that the institute does; and in his speech, he endeavored to show the Commission the importance of establishing a National Board of Health to see that working conditions are sanitary in order to prevent the white plague - tuberculosis, which causes many deaths in the labor ranks each year.
