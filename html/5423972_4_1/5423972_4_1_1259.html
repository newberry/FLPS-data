Albert Flesch, president of the Central Camera Company, 230 South Wabash Avenue, died on June 25.

He was born in Hungary in 1876 and came to Chicago when 14 years old. He had been in business on Wabash Avenue for the past 34 years.

The Central Camera Company is the largest concern of its kind in the Middle West, carrying photographic devices from all parts of the world.

Mr. Flesch served as president and treasurer of the Free Sons of Israel, Lodge I, and he was a member of the North Shore Congregation.
