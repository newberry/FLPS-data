Jewish residents of the West Side met at Porge's Hall, Jefferson and Maxwell streets, Thursday night and organized the Hebrew-American Protective Association of Chicago. The action of nearly 500 citizens who attended the meeting was precipitated by the recent killing of Solomon Optner, a peddler, by John Sheeky. The following permanent officers were elected: president, Dr. Gustavus Black, vice-president, P. Weinshenker, secretary, E. M. Zoline, treasurer, Alderman N. F. Brenner, directors, F. Ziv, R. Rosenberg, S. V. Panama, and S. Brody.
