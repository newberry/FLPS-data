In a short time it is proposed to have all those who have attended the citizenship class, form a "Citizens Club" with the object of taking an interest in civic matters, and assisting its members in becoming Americanized. It is further hoped that many of our foreign brethren will so learn their duties as citizens and help sustain the reputation of the Jews as law-abiding and true Americans.

Mr. Edward E. Baron, a young and promising lawyer who for several years has been doing good work at the Institute in its various departments, is in charge of the Bureau.
