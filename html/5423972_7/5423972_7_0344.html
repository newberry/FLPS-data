The Chicago Hebrew Institute has purchased the Seminary of the Sacred Heart building and the ground at the northwest corner of Taylor and Lytle Streets, 431 by 631 ft; extending to Gilpin Place on the north and Sibley Street on the west, at $110,000. This is one of the historical buildings in the southwestern part of the city. It will now be converted into a Hebrew educational institution. A trust deed has been given back by E. A.
