The last to promise a carload of food for the War sufferers was Mr. Max Karshak, editor of the "Lawyers' Column" in the Courier. On the day following his promise, he already brought to the relief office $200, contributed by the following: Grossman Shoe Co., $100; Karshak and Co., $25; A. E. Kolish, $5; Bernard Levin, $40; Robert S. Cohen, $5; and J. Loebner, $25.
