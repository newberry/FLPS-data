The appeal to the progressive Jewish organizations of Chicago, to come to a Conference, to organize themselves to create immediate relief for the hungry and the weak of Soviet Russia, had a strong response in the hearts of the masses, and a large number of delegates of the various sections of the labor-movement came and unanimously voted to begin the work of conducting a campaign for the relief of Soviet Russia.

Comrade Morris Ziskind called the conference to order, in the name of the Trade Union Conference, and recommended that a Chairman and a Secretary be elected. Comrade L. Benensohn was chosen as Chairman, and M. Ziskind, Secretary.

It was voted that the name of the Conference should be: Chicago's Relief Conference for Soviet Russia.

A credential committee, consisting of Comrades Strausman, Witt, and Fagan, was elected, and reported that the following organizations were represented through their delegates:

An Executive Committee of fifteen was then elected. Following are the officers:

L. Berensohn, Treasurer

Dr. Baumstein, Financial-Secretary

Morris Ziskind, Recording-Secretary

Trustees: Straussman, Witt, Spitolnick, Golden, Bizna, Fagan, Lurie, Dr. Green, Shapiro, Coblents, Weiss, and Blumenthal.

Following this, a collection totaling $177.00 was made.

It was unanimously voted that no organization should conduct its own campaign for Relief-Work in Soviet Russia, without the consent of the Conference.

The Executive Committee of the Conference held its meeting immediately after the conclusion of the General Conference, and elected Sam Golden as Chairman, and M. Ziskind as Secretary.

4

It was decided to publish an appeal in the Yiddish and English press, and Committees were elected to go to the Chicago Federation of Labor and other labor-bodies for this purpose.
