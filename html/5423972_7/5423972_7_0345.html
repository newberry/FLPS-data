Last Monday a meeting of all painters of the city of Chicago was held at the Hebrew Institute.

Dr. Fishkind thanked the painters of Chicago for their noble undertaking, that of painting the Hebrew Institute gratis.

The meeting was conducted to the satisfaction of the officers. An offer of eighty-five days of work for the Institute was the high light of this meeting.

The Painters' Aid Society has taken a very active part in this movement and it is to be hoped that the undertaking will be a success.

A committee was sent out to obtain necessary materials. Work will begin in the near future.

All those who wish to make contributions of time or materials should contact Mr. Lipson at 254 Washburn Avenue, or Mr. Morgolis at 587 W. 13th Street.
