An important meeting of the directors of the Chicago Joint Relief Committee was held last night at the Morrison Hotel. Mr. S. J. Rosenblatt reported on the activity of the Committee, and pointed out that during the past year, the Committee had collected three hundred and ninety thousand dollars. Mr. I. Liederman, secretary of the Committee, read the financial report.

R. B. Mushkin spoke on the subject: "The influence of the Relief upon the synagogues and the influence of the synagogues upon the Relief".

Rabbi Saul Silber discussed "The Cultural Work of the Central Relief in Europe".

Mr. B. Horwich, president of the Joint Relief Committee, in his speech, stressed the urgency of the work in behalf of the refugees and orphans.

Mr. Samuel Neveleff was highly praised at the meeting for his work as manager of the Chicago office of the Joint Relief Committee and for his field work in the middle western states.
