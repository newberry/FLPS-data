p. 1, col. 2.. The second successful presentation of Hall's operetta "Geisha," was given by the Bohemian singing society Dalibor. The society was requested to make this second appearance because of the great number of people that could not be accommodated in its premiere. The premiere should have been held at the Auditorium Theater which is much larger than the Sokol Chicago Hall where the light opera was staged.

Everything turned out brilliantly. The choirs and orchestra were excellent, and the acting and singing was splendid. The attendance was large and responded with a great ovation at the close of the operetta.

Dalibor, the singing society, is very active in Chicago. Among its members are many prominent Bohemian Chicagoans.
