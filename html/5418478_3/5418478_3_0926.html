The Cesky Delnicky Pevecky Sbor (The Bohemian Workingmen's Singing Society) has decided to celebrate its twenty-fiftth year of existence with a popular concert. It will be held on April 16, 1916, in the auditorium of Plzensky Sokol (Pilsen Hall) on Ashland Avenue, near 18th Street, and there will be a small charge for admission. They will, in that way, please not only their music loving friends but also the whole Bohemian public.

At this concert the pupils, of our singing school, in conjunction with the ladies' auxiliary, will sing "Maj" (May).

At this concert gay and lively songs will be sung, because it is admitted that humor is the best antidote in crucial times which Bohemian Chicago now is experiencing.

That the concert will be a great success from a moral standpoint may be \2affirmed by simply mentioning the society's conductor, Mr. J. Houdek.
