A thirty-five week scholarship is offered to young readers of the Skandinaven: six boys and six girls. The girl, in a group of six, who sells the most yearly subscriptions for 1900, will receive the scholarship; the other five girls in the group will receive cash prizes. The boy, in a group of six, who sells the largest number of copies of "Man's Life in the Light of God" will receive a free scholarship; the remaining five will receive cash prizes.

The scholarships are for one whole year at the Albion Academy, in Albion, Wisconsin. [The Albion Academy was a Scandinavian college.]
