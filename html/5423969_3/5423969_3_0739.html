The Sleipner Athletic Society has just celebrated its twenty-fifth anniversary. It was a double festival, so to speak, because the Society at the same time welcomed home the dough boys and Jack Tars, who had just returned.

Sleipner has more than three hundred members and a cash balance in five figures. This is a great change from the days when they met in a basement, and picked coal on the railroad tracks. Most of the members, in the early days, were new- comers, and jobless beside; so, naturally, dues were slow in coming in. It is very different today.
