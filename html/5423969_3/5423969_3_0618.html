The first volume begins the history of the Norwegians in the year 1319 [at the time of the first union with Sweden]. We reprint a paragraph found in the author's introduction:

"The preservation of the people's personal freedom amidst general national decay, the germs of a new development distinctly traceable in social life, have been especially dwelt upon...because they constitute the basis of the Norwegian political and social development in modern times."

In the second volume the immigration [into the United States] is discussed very clearly. The Norwegian pioneer and his exploits, not only on the prairie but also in the cities of the West, are discussed.

The book contains about ninety half-tone plates.
