The Thora Ladies' Society [also known as Women's Lodge, Thora] celebrated the twenty-fifth anniversary of its founding last Thursday in the Danish Brotherhood Hall, 317 Hirsch Street, near California Avenue. The president, Mrs. Marie Blom, introduced Dr. A. H. Olsen who congratulated the Society on its anniversary. Dr. Olsen is the Society's doctor.

Two of the charter members, Mrs. Olivia Blackstad and Mrs. Ragina Anderson, were made honorary members. Mr. J. H. Blackstad read the history of the Society. The highlights of the activities of the Society were as follows:

Twenty-five years ago the Thora Society was organized, October 9, 1883. It was organized by a committee set up by the Scandinavian Workers' Society. The committee consisted of the following: J. H. Blackstad, Christian Lindblom, 2and Hans Holstad. The following committee wrote the constitution and bylaws: Mrs. Olivia Blackstad, Mrs. [gap] Lenterberg (now Arveson), Mrs. Hermanda Johnson, Miss Johanne [gap], and Miss Diana Hober.

The first president was Mrs. Martin Fjeld. The following members have been presidents of the Society: Mrs. Olivia Blackstad. Mrs. Anna Rasmussen, Mrs. Johanna [gap], Mrs. Julia Carsen, Mrs. Johanne Nielsen, Mrs. [gap] Stether Runden, Mrs. Enga Steffens, Mrs. Christine Berg, and Mrs. M. Blom.

The Society's physicians have been: Dr. [gap] Dr. J. H. Lee, and at present, Dr. A. H. Olsen.

The present executives are as follows: Mrs. Marie Blom, president: Mrs. O. Blackstad, vice-president; Mrs. Marie Mielsen, secretary; Mrs. Evelyn [gap], financial secretary; Mrs. Anette Haugan, treasurer; Mrs. Christine 3Dybdahl, marshall; Mrs. Alvilde Olsen, doorkeeper; Mrs. [gap] Heberg, librarian; Mrs. Christine Soderlund, Mrs. Anna [gap], and Mrs. Marie Craderi, trustees. Delegates to the Norwegian National Committee are Mrs. Marie [gap], and Mrs. Enga Steffens.

The Society has 235 members and pays sick and death benefits.
