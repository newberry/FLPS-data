The Scandinavian Medical Society held its first annual meeting last Thursday. Since the president, Dr. Svenning Dahl, was out of town, the vice-president opened the meeting.

Dr. C.F. Klaas gave an excellent talk on obstetrics, and pointed out several bad practices of the modern physician. Dr. H.C. Hanson told of several humorous incidents he experienced on this subject. Dr. Thomas Warloe spoke on asepsis and antisepsis. The discussion centered mostly on confinement cases and pregnancy.
